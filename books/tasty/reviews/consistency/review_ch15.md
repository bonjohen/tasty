# Setting Consistency Review — Chapter 15: The Body Remembers

**Chapter:** 15 (Section 7, Ch 15–16)
**POV:** Hurkuzak
**Setting:** Pre-industrial dark fantasy — wagons, sleds, bladed weapons, bows, traps; no engines, firearms, or electricity
**Reviewed against:** `.claude/rules/poor-consistency.md`, `book/CLAUDE.md`, `book/docs/section_07_outline.md`

---

## Issue-by-Issue Findings

---

### Issue 1

**Passage:**
> "The strength was Hakiia's kind — dense, explosive, built for application rather than endurance. This was installed."

**Problem:** Modern technical register — "installed" as a metaphor for bodily transformation.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Installed" belongs to mechanical and later technological discourse — equipment fitted into a machine, software loaded onto hardware. The metaphor frames Hurkuzak's body as a device receiving components. No character in a pre-industrial world without engines or industrial manufacture would reach for this word as a natural mental image. The world of this novel has no machinery context from which to draw the metaphor.

**Better direction:** Stay in the register the chapter already uses elsewhere — weight, material, inheritance, presence. "This was not his. It had come from somewhere else." Or: "The strength was Hakiia's kind — dense, explosive, built for violence, and none of it originally his." The chapter's own language of "specific" and "borrowed" works; "installed" is a step too far into mechanical metaphor.

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 2

**Passage:**
> "He reached inside himself and found someone else's hand already there."

**Problem:** Metaphor that borders on clinical psychological language.

**Why it breaks immersion:** This is marginally acceptable as poetic language, but "reached inside himself" is a modern introspective idiom — the kind of phrasing common in contemporary self-help discourse and psychological fiction. In a character whose culture has no tradition of psychological self-examination as a named practice, this metaphor may signal the author's frame rather than the character's. It also anthropomorphizes the interior too cleanly for what is meant to feel uncanny.

**Better direction:** The chapter handles the same idea more effectively elsewhere through externalized wrongness — the hand arriving early, the grip being different. The interior moment would land harder if it stayed similarly concrete and slightly alien: "Something else had already taken its place in the spaces he thought were his." The image of a hand inside himself is almost too comprehensible for this stage of the Bleed.

**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 3

**Passage:**

**No issue found here.** This is precisely the right register — specific, material, pre-industrial, practical. Noted as a positive example of the chapter at its best.

### Issue 4

**Passage:**
> "The fire responded immediately — the draw improved, the flame steadied, and the heat output changed from a broad, unfocused warmth to something directed and efficient."

**Problem:** "Heat output" carries mild engineering register.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Output" in the sense of quantified energy production belongs to industrial and scientific discourse. A person in a pre-industrial world would feel that the fire gave more heat, gave better heat, burned truer — not that its "output" changed. This is a small drift but perceptible in a chapter otherwise so careful about register.

**Better direction:** "...the draft pulled clean, the flame settled, and the heat it gave changed from a broad, unfocused warmth to something directed and steady." "Gave" rather than "output" keeps the sentence in the right register without sacrificing precision.

**Priority:** High

### Issue 5

**Passage:**
> "The corrections had gone quiet. Not absent — present but silent. The difference between an empty room and one with someone in it who has chosen not to speak."

**No issue found here.** The register is clean and fits the world. The observation is felt rather than categorized. Strong.

### Issue 6

**Passage:**
> "He was not hearing a voice. He was hearing himself, changed. The person who had killed and consumed and survived was not the person who had fumbled the elk shot."

**Problem:** "Fumbled the elk shot" is tonally flat and slightly idiomatic in a way that risks modernizing.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Fumbled" is not inherently modern, but the phrase "fumbled the elk shot" sounds close to sports-register casualness — "fumbled the catch," "fumbled the play." In a world of bows and traps rather than rifles, "shot" itself is not wrong (arrows are shot), but the combination with "fumbled" creates a mildly colloquial ring that undercuts the gravity of the surrounding prose.

**Better direction:** "...not the person who had misjudged the elk, who had hesitated when Hakiia would have drawn." Or simply: "...not the person who had let the elk walk." This preserves the self-criticism while staying in the chapter's more formal, weighted register.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 7

**Passage:**
> "The rational explanation held."

**Problem:** "Rational" as a self-applied category.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Rational" is a post-Enlightenment philosophical category — part of a discourse about reason, rationalism, and rational thought that developed in a specific intellectual history. A pre-industrial character, especially one from a demon culture with its own cosmology and taboo logic, would not naturally frame his self-justification as the "rational explanation." He might think: "The explanation held." Or: "The reason held. Mostly." The word "rational" is the author's vocabulary, not this world's.

**Better direction:** "The explanation held." The sentence that follows — "But the corrections had a quality..." — already delivers the doubt. The word "rational" adds a modern logical frame that is unnecessary and slightly out of register.

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 8

**Passage:**
> "These arrived straight — conclusions without the preceding argument."

**Problem:** Mildly formal logical vocabulary.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "The preceding argument" is closer to academic or legal register than to a pre-industrial character's self-narration. It is not severely anachronistic, but it lands slightly above the register the rest of the chapter maintains. Hurkuzak has been rendered as intuitive, physical, and concrete in his thinking — this phrase sounds like a trained debater evaluating his own cognition.

**Better direction:** "These arrived whole — finished, with no thinking that led to them." Or: "They came already decided." Keeps the same idea in a register truer to how Hurkuzak is written throughout.

**Priority:** Low

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 15 is a disciplined, well-controlled piece of writing with strong handling of the pre-industrial setting in most of its specifics. The physical world — fire-banking technique, the compacted snow, the water pot, the fallen tree — is rendered in the correct register throughout. The Bleed's first appearances are handled with exactly the right level of constraint: terse, unmarked, practical, and consistent with a hostile survival voice rather than an identifiable personality.

The chapter's consistency problems are narrow and specific: two or three word choices drift into mechanical, engineering, or post-Enlightenment intellectual register ("installed," "heat output," "rational"). These are author-vocabulary intrusions rather than systemic worldbuilding failures. The narrative voice is otherwise convincingly native to the world.

The single most disruptive issue is "installed," which imports a hardware/software metaphor into a moment of high emotional significance. "Heat output" and "rational" are quieter breaks but still worth correcting.

## Pattern Summary

The chapter's recurring weakness is a single type of drift: the author's modern frame of reference appears in moments when Hurkuzak is processing his own transformation analytically. The physical and sensory prose stays in the correct register throughout. The drift occurs specifically at moments of self-reflection — when Hurkuzak watches himself change and reaches for a vocabulary to name what is happening. At those moments, the author's own conceptual vocabulary (installation metaphors, engineering precision, Enlightenment logical categories) bleeds into the character's interiority. The fix in every case is the same: replace the analyzing word with a felt or material description, and trust the reader to understand.

No cultural anachronisms. No modern social assumptions. No misplaced objects or technology. No dialogue that modernizes. The world is consistently pre-industrial. The drift is entirely in the register of introspective analysis.

## Revision Plan

**Priority 1 (High — fix all three):**
1. Replace "This was installed." — the most disruptive anachronism
2. Replace "heat output" with "heat it gave" or equivalent in-register phrasing
3. Replace "The rational explanation held." with "The explanation held."

**Priority 2 (Medium — recommended):**
4. Revise "fumbled the elk shot" to stay in the chapter's weighted register
5. Consider revising "reached inside himself and found someone else's hand already there" — not essential but the image of finding an interior hand is on the edge of modern psychological idiom

**Priority 3 (Low — optional polish):**
6. Revise "conclusions without the preceding argument" to something more vernacular and physical

## Root Cause Note

This chapter is not failing from isolated random word choices, deeper worldbuilding inconsistency, or narrator-voice drift in a structural sense. The problem is specific and consistent: a small set of analytical and metaphorical vocabulary that belongs to the author's modern technical and intellectual register intrudes at moments of introspective or evaluative narration. The worldbuilding itself is solid. The narrator's default voice is correctly calibrated. The fixes are surface-level and targeted — three to five word-or-phrase substitutions will close all High and Medium issues without touching the chapter's structure, pacing, or prose style.
