# Weak-Writing Review: Chapter 18 — The Hunter's Patience

Reviewed against `.claude/rules/weak-writing.md`. Chapter is intentionally written in a weighted, restrained literary register; flagged items below are genuinely weak by the rule's criteria, not stylistic choices.

---

## Per-Issue Findings

### Issue 1
**Quote:** "Koss had paid above the agreed price without complaint, which meant the final buyer had paid considerably more, which meant the product had been exceptional and Vark's cut had been, as always, a fraction of what it generated downstream."

**Problem:** Run-on, chained "which meant" clauses; the same logical step is restated twice.

**Why it weakens:** The cascading clauses turn a sharp economic observation into a slow, breathy paraphrase of itself. The reader gets the same insight three times.

**Suggested revision:** Cut to two beats. "Koss had paid above the agreed price without complaint. That meant the final buyer had paid considerably more, and Vark's cut, as always, was a fraction of what the body generated downstream."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 2
**Quote:** "She did not resent the math. Her work sat at the base of it — risk and labor with no claim on what came after. The profits climbed with each set of hands the product passed through."

**Problem:** Paragraph restates Issue 1 in different words. Repetition of point already made.

**Why it weakens:** The previous paragraph has just walked the reader through exactly this. Restating it as principle is unnecessary explanation.

**Suggested revision:** Cut the middle sentence. Keep "She did not resent the math" and the equipment line.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 3
**Quote:** "operating through intermediaries whose names were kept out of it — money that moved without a face attached."

**Problem:** Two phrasings of the same idea separated by a dash.

**Why it weakens:** "Names kept out of it" and "money that moved without a face" are the same observation. The dash-clause is a gloss.

**Suggested revision:** Pick one. "operating through intermediaries — money that moved without a face attached."

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 4
**Quote:** "Unusual was a professional word. In Vark's trade it meant worth money. Significant money."

**Problem:** Three sentences for one beat; the third ("Significant money.") is throat-clearing emphasis.

**Why it weakens:** The fragment exists only to underline the prior line. The line is already clear.

**Suggested revision:** "Unusual was a professional word. In Vark's trade it meant money."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 5
**Quote:** "She had traveled east through the passes as the snow receded, on foot because the terrain was impassable to horses and because her body was a better instrument than any animal she could ride."

**Problem:** Wordy compound reason; "because... and because..." stacked.

**Why it weakens:** The two "because" clauses fight for emphasis and dilute both. The body-as-instrument observation is the stronger one and should stand.

**Suggested revision:** "She had traveled east through the passes on foot as the snow receded. The terrain refused horses, and her body was the better instrument anyway."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 6
**Quote:** "She was in the settlement three days before Hurkuzak arrived. She did not see him. She heard about him days later, through the talk that travels ahead of strangers on mountain roads — mention passed from the trading post through two settlements before it reached her at a cook fire."

**Problem:** "Three days before... days later" creates a small temporal contradiction (she was there first, but heard "days later"). Also, the route of the rumor is over-specified.

**Why it weakens:** Pulls the reader out to reconcile timing; the chain of settlements is texture the chapter does not need.

**Suggested revision:** "She had been in the settlement three days before Hurkuzak arrived, and never saw him. She heard about him later, through the talk that travels ahead of strangers on mountain roads."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 7
**Quote:** "He had been gaunt in the face but strong in the body. He had moved strangely — too fast for his condition, too aware. The woman could not tell whether his alertness was fear or threat."

**Problem:** Solid in itself, but the next paragraph ("One mountain man was not a lead. One with a body that contradicted his condition was. The contradiction — gaunt face, strong body — was the specific mark...") restates "gaunt face, strong body" a third time inside a few lines.

**Why it weakens:** Repetition of the exact phrasing across consecutive paragraphs.

**Suggested revision:** Drop the parenthetical "— gaunt face, strong body —" in the following paragraph. The reader has it.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 8
**Quote:** "They had approached her at a campsite with the confidence of men who saw a woman alone and calculated their odds from the outside."

**Problem:** "Calculated their odds from the outside" is vague — the meaning is doing geometry the reader has to redo.

**Why it weakens:** The image is not concrete. "From the outside" could mean several things and lands on none.

**Suggested revision:** "They had approached her at a campsite with the confidence of men who had counted only what they could see."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 9
**Quote:** "The first one reached for her arm and she broke his wrist with a rotation that used his own grip as the lever. The second drew a knife and she took it from him with a speed that left his hand empty and his mind several seconds behind the event."

**Problem:** Two consecutive sentences, identical structure ("The first... and she..." / "The second... and she..."), both ending in a long descriptive tail. Monotone rhythm at a moment that should snap.

**Why it weakens:** The parallel construction makes the violence feel staged and slow. A fight beat needs varied length.

**Suggested revision:** Vary length. "The first reached for her arm. She broke his wrist with a rotation that used his own grip as the lever. The second drew a knife; she took it from him before his mind caught up to his hand."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 10
**Quote:** "The wrist she had broken would heal badly. She did not care about it."

**Problem:** "She did not care about it" — flat tell. The behavioral evidence is right above.

**Why it weakens:** The previous sentences (she ate her meal; they left) already render her indifference. The final sentence states what the action shows.

**Suggested revision:** Cut "She did not care about it." Let "She ate her meal. The wrist she had broken would heal badly." carry it.

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 11
**Quote:** "She continued east. The contract's scope was broad enough to accommodate her own inquiries, which ran parallel to the contract's goals and occasionally intersected."

**Problem:** Wordy and abstract. "Ran parallel... occasionally intersected" is bureaucratic.

**Why it weakens:** The voice slips from her body into a summary of her arrangements. The information is structural and could be tighter.

**Suggested revision:** "She continued east. The contract's scope was broad enough to cover her own questions."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 12
**Quote:** "It was on a logging trail east of the settlement where Hurkuzak had been reported, and it was wrong."

**Problem:** Throat-clearing "It was... and it was..." opening. Two empty "it was" subjects in one sentence.

**Why it weakens:** The reveal — wrong — gets buried inside throat-clearing.

**Suggested revision:** "She found it on a logging trail east of the settlement where Hurkuzak had been reported. The track was wrong."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 13
**Quote:** "The depth was excessive for the width — the body that made it weighed more than its frame suggested. Dense muscle. A man who was more than a man."

**Problem:** "A man who was more than a man" is the kind of genre-default flourish CLAUDE.md specifically warns against.

**Why it weakens:** It states the implication the previous two beats already deliver. Telegraphs and editorializes.

**Suggested revision:** Cut "A man who was more than a man." End on "Dense muscle."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 14
**Quote:** "The track read like a hunter's. The understanding was in the feet, not the eyes."

**Problem:** The second sentence is gnomic in a way that doesn't earn its line. It tries to be aphorism and lands as filler.

**Why it weakens:** Strains for effect without adding information; the previous sentence is sharper alone.

**Suggested revision:** Cut the second sentence.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 15
**Quote:** "Vark followed the track for half a day. It led east through the forest, staying below the ridgelines, avoiding open ground, choosing routes that minimized visibility from above. The route selection was tactical — the deliberate line of someone who expected to be followed."

**Problem:** "The route selection was tactical" tells what the previous sentence has already shown.

**Why it weakens:** The list (below ridgelines, avoiding open ground, minimizing visibility) is the tactical evidence. Naming it again is redundant.

**Suggested revision:** "Vark followed the track for half a day. It led east through the forest, staying below the ridgelines, avoiding open ground, choosing routes that minimized visibility from above — the deliberate line of someone who expected to be followed."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 16
**Quote:** "She found a campsite where the track paused overnight. Cold fire, no shelter, minimal disturbance of the ground. He had slept — the compression in the earth said so — but he had slept lightly and on his side, facing the direction of approach."

**Problem:** "He had slept — the compression in the earth said so — but he had slept lightly" repeats "he had slept" with an interrupting parenthetical.

**Why it weakens:** Stutter. The parenthetical hands the reader the proof, then the sentence backs up to the verb.

**Suggested revision:** "He had slept lightly, on his side, facing the direction of approach — the compression in the earth said so."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 17
**Quote:** "She found the place where he had eaten — a ring of cleaned bone from a rabbit, caught and consumed on the trail. Rabbits in this terrain required either snares — she found no evidence of snares — or speed beyond the ordinary."

**Problem:** Two em-dashes in one short passage; "snares... no evidence of snares" repeats the noun.

**Why it weakens:** Punctuation overuse and a clumsy snare-snare echo.

**Suggested revision:** "Rabbits in this terrain required snares. She found none. That left speed beyond the ordinary."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 18
**Quote:** "She knelt beside the fire ring and thought."

**Problem:** "And thought" is throat-clearing and tells.

**Why it weakens:** The next paragraph is the thinking. The verb adds nothing.

**Suggested revision:** "She knelt beside the fire ring."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 19
**Quote:** "The track was demon. The route selection was trained. The campcraft was disciplined. The physical signs — stride, depth, the speed implied by the rabbit catch — said more than a standard demon body could account for. And the direction was east, which was the direction the contract's money had pointed her, which was the direction the premium buyer Koss had mentioned in the winter wanted material from."

**Problem:** Final sentence is a chained "which was the direction... which was the direction..." run-on. Three of the same construction.

**Why it weakens:** The triple "the direction" hammer flattens what should be the chapter's pivot.

**Suggested revision:** "And the direction was east — where the contract's money had pointed her, where Koss had said in the winter the premium buyers wanted material from."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 20
**Quote:** "She had used that technique herself, in the field, when she needed marrow without tools. It was a hunter's technique, not a demon's."

**Problem:** "It was a hunter's technique, not a demon's" — explanatory tag after the showing already happened.

**Why it weakens:** The first sentence has placed Vark and the tracked figure side by side. The second sentence interprets the comparison out loud.

**Suggested revision:** Cut the second sentence.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 21
**Quote:** "She set the bone down. The picture didn't fit a standard borderland demon — too skilled, too careful. Not a feral either, which would have been messier. And the signs of it were wrong for anything she had tracked out of the ordinary trade. This one moved differently — the capabilities felt learned rather than inherited. As though the body's abilities had come recently and the mind had not yet caught up to them."

**Problem:** Six sentences saying one thing: this isn't a normal demon. The paragraph below ("There was a category for this...") would land harder if this one were two sentences.

**Why it weakens:** The paragraph reaches the same observation four ways before moving on. Continues after the point has been made.

**Suggested revision:** "She set the bone down. The picture didn't fit a standard borderland demon — too skilled, too careful, the body's abilities arriving faster than the mind could account for them."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 22
**Quote:** "What she was assembling was not a standard borderland kill. The capabilities had arrived recently — the body's knowledge outrunning the mind's adjustment. The profile was specific enough that buyers of a certain class paid in advance to be told if it surfaced. She had a buyer of that class. She had a number he had offered."

**Problem:** "The capabilities had arrived recently — the body's knowledge outrunning the mind's adjustment" is the third repetition of this exact image (Issue 21 had it; the paragraph before that had it).

**Why it weakens:** The chapter has now told the reader three times that the body knows more than the mind. Once is striking. Three flattens it.

**Suggested revision:** Cut the second sentence here. Open: "What she was assembling was not a standard borderland kill. The profile was specific enough that buyers of a certain class paid in advance to be told if it surfaced. She had a buyer of that class. She had a number he had offered."

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 23
**Quote:** "She stood. She checked her equipment — the crossbow, the bolts, the blade, the gear she carried for exactly this kind of extended pursuit. Everything was in order. It was always in order."

**Problem:** "It was always in order" is restated emphasis.

**Why it weakens:** "Everything was in order" already does the work. The repetition tries to add weight and instead deflates.

**Suggested revision:** "She stood. She checked her equipment — crossbow, bolts, blade, the gear for an extended pursuit. Everything was in order, as it always was."

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 24
**Quote:** "She looked east. The track continued, clear enough in the soft spring earth that following it would require attention but not difficulty."

**Problem:** "Would require attention but not difficulty" is bureaucratic phrasing.

**Why it weakens:** Abstract noun construction where a verb would land harder.

**Suggested revision:** "She looked east. The track held clear in the soft spring earth — easy enough to follow, hard enough to require attention."

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 25
**Quote:** "The pieces had stopped adding up to nothing and started adding up to something. She did not yet know what she was following. But it was nothing she had tracked before."

**Problem:** "Adding up to nothing... adding up to something" is a thin construction; the second sentence and third sentence repeat the same admission.

**Why it weakens:** Three sentences telling the reader the same thing — that something has shifted and she doesn't yet know what.

**Suggested revision:** "The pieces had stopped adding up to nothing. She did not yet know what she was following, only that she had not tracked it before."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 26
**Quote:** "The forest received her without preference. The track led east. She followed."

**Problem:** "The track led east. She followed." duplicates the line two paragraphs above ("She looked east. The track continued..."). Plus "Vark started walking" immediately above.

**Why it weakens:** Three near-identical departure beats in eight lines: she stood, she started walking, she followed. The chapter ends with the same image three times.

**Suggested revision:** Cut "Vark started walking" or fold the closing into a single beat. Keep "The forest received her without preference" as the ending image.

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 27
**Quote:** "The light was good. The terrain was navigable."

**Problem:** "The terrain was navigable" is flat and abstract; "navigable" is the wrong register.

**Why it weakens:** Bureaucratic adjective at a moment that wants sensory weight. The next sentence (wet earth, pine, scent) is doing the actual work.

**Suggested revision:** "The light was good. The ground gave under her boots."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 28
**Quote:** "the faint trace of something not quite human — a scent she could follow the way a dog follows blood, without effort, through the body alone."

**Problem:** "The way a dog follows blood" is close to cliche, and "without effort, through the body alone" piles two qualifiers on the same image.

**Why it weakens:** The simile is a familiar one. The trailing qualifier softens the line that should close the chapter.

**Suggested revision:** "the faint trace of something not quite human — a scent her body would follow without thought."

**Priority:** Medium

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 18 is structurally sound and tonally consistent with the book's voice, but it suffers from one recurring weakness: it repeats its own observations. Vark sees a thing, the prose names the thing, then a sentence later the prose names it again as principle, and a paragraph later the principle is restated as conclusion. The "body's knowledge outrunning the mind's adjustment" image appears three times. The "gaunt face, strong body" pair appears three times. The departure beat at the end happens three times. The chapter is also studded with small throat-clearing tags ("and thought," "It was always in order," "She did not care about it," "A man who was more than a man") that interpret what the action has already shown. Cleaned of these, it would land harder and read shorter without losing a single observation.

## Top 10 Problems by Impact

1. **Repetition of "body's knowledge outrunning the mind"** across three paragraphs (Issues 21, 22)
2. **Triple departure ending** — "Vark started walking," "She followed," and "The track led east" all do the same beat (Issue 26)
3. **Run-on "which was the direction... which was the direction..."** at the chapter's pivot (Issue 19)
4. **"A man who was more than a man"** — genre-default flourish that telegraphs (Issue 13)
5. **"It was on a logging trail... and it was wrong"** — throat-clearing on a key reveal (Issue 12)
6. **Monotone rhythm in the fight beat** — two parallel sentences flatten a snap moment (Issue 9)
7. **"She did not care about it"** tells what the action shows (Issue 10)
8. **"Gaunt face, strong body" repeated three times** in adjacent paragraphs (Issue 7)
9. **Six-sentence "this isn't a normal demon" paragraph** continues past its point (Issue 21)
10. **Wordy "which meant... which meant..." cascade** about the flesh economy (Issue 1)

## Pattern Summary

Three recurring weaknesses:

- **Show, then tell.** Vark observes something concrete; the next sentence labels what she has just observed ("The route selection was tactical." "It was a hunter's technique, not a demon's." "She did not care about it.")
- **Three-beat repetition.** Important images get said once, restated, then summarized. "Body outrunning mind" three times. "Gaunt face strong body" three times. The departure three times.
- **Throat-clearing fragments.** Short emphasis sentences ("Significant money." "It was always in order." "A man who was more than a man.") try to add weight after a line that already had it.

The core skill — the dense observational prose, the procedural patience of Vark's thinking — is intact. The problem is the chapter doesn't trust itself, so it explains what it has already shown.
