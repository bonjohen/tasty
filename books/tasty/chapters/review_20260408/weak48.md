# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 48: What Was Passed On

## Per-Issue Findings

### 1. Redundant cataloguing of healed wounds (Medium)
Quote: "The bolt wound in his shoulder was a dimple of scar tissue that ached in cold weather and did not ache in warm weather."
Problem: The "ached / did not ache" antithesis is filler — the second clause adds nothing. Mild wordiness.
Revision: "The bolt wound in his shoulder was a dimple of scar tissue that ached in cold weather."
Priority: Medium.

### 2. Throat-clearing meta-sentence (Medium)
Quote: "There was a point at which a man had to accept that he moved through the world and the world was changed by his moving through it, and that the change did not always take the shape he intended or could foresee."
Problem: Abstract, sermonizing, run-on. Explains the theme rather than letting the bite carry it.
Revision: Tighten: "A man moved through the world, and the world was changed by his moving through it. The change did not always take the shape he intended."
Priority: Medium.

### 3. Over-explained "searching for a name" (Medium)
Quote: "He had spent a long time looking for the name of what he was now, and then one morning he had stopped looking, not because he had found the name but because he had understood that the searching was costing him mornings he could be spending on bread and sun and the ordinary lives of people who had nothing to do with him."
Problem: One long sentence saying a simple thing twice. The "bread and sun" phrase is already used as imagery elsewhere; here it dilutes.
Revision: Compress: "He had spent a long time looking for the name of what he was now. One morning he stopped looking. The searching was costing him mornings."
Priority: Medium.

### 4. Telling the war-that-didn't-come (Low)
Quote: "They were not at war. That was the discovery that had taken longest — he had been braced for war..."
Problem: Slightly explanatory; acceptable as interiority. Leave.
Priority: Low.

### 5. Redundant uncertainty list (Medium)
Quote: "He did not know what the bite had passed on. One voice or two. The brothers as they had been, or the composite they had become. Something recognizable or something changed beyond recognition by the passage through a new body. He had no way to know and no means of finding out."
Problem: "He had no way to know and no means of finding out" repeats itself. Doublet.
Revision: Cut to "He had no way to know."
Priority: Medium.

### 6. "did not move and did not breathe" doublet (Low)
Quote: "She held still and did not move and did not breathe and waited..."
Problem: "Held still" already contains "did not move." Mild redundancy — but the incantatory rhythm is intentional. Leave.
Priority: Low.

### 7. Overwritten run-on describing the two presences (High)
Quote: "...one with a quality of roughness and one with a quality of stillness, one that pressed forward against the front of her perception like something lean and impatient, and one that waited."
Problem: "Quality of roughness / quality of stillness" is abstract throat-clearing. The concrete images ("lean and impatient," "waited") do the work. Cut the abstractions.
Revision: "...distinct, layered — one lean and impatient, pressing forward against the front of her perception; one that waited."
Priority: High.

### 8. Triple "She had no..." anaphora bloated (Medium)
Quote: "She had no name for what she was hearing. She had no frame for two presences sharing the space where her thoughts had always been alone. She had no language for the fact that they were aware of her..."
Problem: Three near-synonyms (name / frame / language) for the same idea. The third is the strongest because it pivots into their awareness; the first two are throat-clearing.
Revision: Drop the first two; keep "She had no language for the fact that they were aware of her..."
Priority: Medium.

### 9. "for the very first time" intensifier (Medium)
Quote: "...and who was hearing it, in turn, for the very first time."
Problem: "Very" is an empty intensifier. "First time" is sufficient.
Revision: "...and who was hearing it, in turn, for the first time."
Priority: Medium.

### 10. Overstated closing image (Low)
Quote: "...one more thing arriving from somewhere else with nowhere left to go."
Problem: Slightly strains; but functions as the chapter's closing chord. Leave.
Priority: Low.

### 11. "the way she found herself wet when the tide came in too fast and she had not moved quickly enough" (Medium)
Problem: Long subordinate tail. The simile is fine; the qualifier "and she had not moved quickly enough" is filler that explains the obvious.
Revision: "...the way she found herself wet when the tide came in too fast."
Priority: Medium.

---

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 48 is a quiet, careful coda. Its strengths are restraint, image, and the calm pre-industrial register the book has earned. Its weaknesses are localized: a handful of doublets, one overwritten metaphysical run-on, and two passages where abstraction (a man had to accept... / a quality of...) does work that the concrete images already do. No structural problems. The chapter lands; it just needs a few cuts to stop apologizing for its own meaning.

## Top 10 Ranked

1. Overwritten "quality of roughness/stillness" run-on (High)
2. Sermonizing "There was a point at which a man had to accept..." (Medium)
3. "Looking for the name" sentence over-explains (Medium)
4. Redundant uncertainty doublet "no way to know and no means of finding out" (Medium)
5. Triple "She had no name / frame / language" anaphora (Medium)
6. Healed-wound "ached / did not ache" doublet (Medium)
7. "the very first time" intensifier (Medium)
8. Tide simile tail filler (Medium)
9. "did not move and did not breathe" mild redundancy (Low)
10. Closing "nowhere left to go" mild strain (Low)

## Pattern Summary

The author's recurring tic in this chapter is the doublet — saying the same thing in two clauses linked by "and" or by abstract restatement. A second pattern: leaning on abstract nouns ("quality of," "the fact that") when the concrete image just before or after already carries the meaning.

## Revision Plan

1. Cut the abstract halves of the two-presence description; keep the concrete images.
2. Tighten the sermonizing "a man had to accept" sentence.
3. Compress the "looking for the name" sentence.
4. Drop the "no means of finding out" half of the doublet.
5. Drop the first two "She had no..." restatements.
6. Trim the warm-weather tail and the tide-simile tail.
7. Cut "very" from "the very first time."
