# Weak-Writing Review: Chapter 15 — The Body Remembers

Reviewed against `.claude/rules/weak-writing.md`. The book's deliberate voice (weighted, atmospheric, McCarthy/Morrison-leaning, restrained horror, unmarked Bleed) is preserved; only prose that is genuinely weak by the rule's criteria is flagged.

---

## Per-Issue Findings

### Issue 1
**Quote:** "The motion was small — a stretch from the sleeping platform to the wall where the hand axe hung on its peg. The distance was familiar. This time the hand arrived early. Not by much. A fraction of a breath. But the hand he had carried his entire life did not move that way."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing / over-explanation. The first sentence ("He reached for the axe and his hand arrived before he expected it") already lands the beat. The next two sentences ("The motion was small...The distance was familiar") restate the obvious before the prose gets back to work.
**Why it weakens:** The reader does not need the geography of the gesture spelled out; they need the strangeness. The clarification dilutes the opening's spring.
**Suggested revision:** Cut "The distance was familiar." Tighten to: "The motion was small — a stretch from the sleeping platform to the wall where the axe hung. This time the hand arrived early. Not by much. A fraction of a breath. But the hand he had carried his entire life did not move that way."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 2
**Quote:** "He held the axe and looked at his hand on the handle. The grip was different. Not in its position — he was holding the tool the same way he had always held it. The difference was underneath — in the density of the fingers, the way the hand closed around the wood with a sureness that was not his."
**Problem:** Wordiness and redundancy. "Not in its position — he was holding the tool the same way he had always held it" says the same thing twice in a single sentence.
**Why it weakens:** Two clauses defining the negative when one would do. The dash construction is doing extra work it does not need.
**Suggested revision:** "The grip was different. Not the position — he held the tool the way he always had. The difference was underneath: in the density of the fingers, in the way the hand closed around the wood with a sureness that was not his."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 3
**Quote:** "But the wrongnesses continued."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing transition + abstraction. "Wrongnesses" is vague where the chapter has been concrete. The "But" opener telegraphs.
**Why it weakens:** The line stalls between scenes and tells the reader what to think next instead of showing it.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the line entirely, or replace with a concrete bridge. Best: cut.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 4
**Quote:** "He stood that afternoon and the cabin felt smaller. His body was occupying the space differently."
**Problem:** Weak verb construction ("was occupying"). The progressive distances the action.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence describes inhabitation in the slack tense most likely to drain it of presence.
**Suggested revision:** "He stood that afternoon and the cabin felt smaller. His body occupied the space differently."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 5
**Quote:** "The snow had compacted in the weeks since the last storm, and the surface held his weight in ways it had not held it before."
**Problem:** Wordy and repeats the verb ("held his weight in ways it had not held it"). Awkward.
**Why it weakens:** The repetition is not rhetorical, just clumsy. Slows a sentence that should land.
**Suggested revision:** "The snow had compacted since the last storm, and the surface held his weight as it had not before."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 6
**Quote:** "He walked to the tree line and the walking was easy in a way it should not have been."
**Problem:** Nominalization ("the walking was easy") and a vague qualifier ("in a way it should not have been").
**Why it weakens:** "Walking" as noun is weaker than the verb it just used. The hedged tail flattens what should feel uncanny.
**Suggested revision:** "He walked to the tree line. It came easy in a way it should not have."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 7
**Quote:** "His breathing was controlled."
**Problem:** Passive/static construction with weak linking verb. Telling not showing.
**Why it weakens:** A flat report between two stronger sentences. Reads as filler.
**Suggested revision:** "His breath came even." Or fold into the next sentence.
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 8
**Quote:** "He tested himself. Not deliberately — the testing came from curiosity that he could not suppress, a need to know where the edges of the new capacity lay."
**Problem:** Over-explanation. The narrator interrupts the scene to define what kind of testing it is and why. "A need to know where the edges of the new capacity lay" is the kind of summary the chapter has earned the right to skip.
**Why it weakens:** Stalls the lift of the log. The reader will infer the involuntary curiosity from the act itself.
**Suggested revision:** "He tested himself. Not deliberately — the curiosity moved him before he could refuse it."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 9
**Quote:** "It came up — not with casual power, but with a strength his arms should not have contained. The trunk was thick and wet with freeze and should have been beyond him. The strength that lifted it was specific. It gathered and fired rather than sustained — it wanted application, had no patience for endurance."
**Problem:** Saying the same point three times. "Strength his arms should not have contained" → "should have been beyond him" → "the strength that lifted it was specific" all circle the same observation before the genuinely new content (gather/fire) arrives.
**Why it weakens:** The paragraph stalls. Repetition deadens the strangeness.
**Suggested revision:** "It came up — not with casual power, but with a strength his arms should not have contained. The trunk was thick and frost-wet and should have been beyond him. The strength was specific: it gathered and fired rather than sustained, wanted application, had no patience for endurance."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 10
**Quote:** "He had felt this quality once before, from the outside, watching someone else carry it. He set the trunk down and did not follow the thought further."
**Problem:** "From the outside" is mildly throat-clearing; "did not follow the thought further" is a near-cliche of evasion writing. The first half can be tighter.
**Why it weakens:** The second sentence's evasion is doing useful work for the Bleed; the first sentence dilutes the recognition with extra prepositional phrases.
**Suggested revision:** "He had felt this quality once before, watching someone else carry it. He set the trunk down and did not follow the thought further."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 11
**Quote:** "He stood in the snow and listened. The forest was making its winter sounds — the creak of branches under ice, the settling of snow, the small movements of unseen things in the underbrush."
**Problem:** "Was making" is weak progressive. The framing sentence ("forest was making its winter sounds") tells before the catalog shows.
**Why it weakens:** Two sentences where one would do, and the second one is the weaker construction.
**Suggested revision:** "He stood in the snow and listened. The forest made its winter sounds — the creak of branches under ice, the settling of snow, the small movements of unseen things in the underbrush."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 12
**Quote:** "He ate that evening. The alcove was nearly empty. The last whole meal was close. He opened the door and stood in the cold air of it for a moment, and the cold air was not only cold."
**Problem:** Repetition of "cold air" within one sentence; the construction "the cold air of it" is awkward and unclear in pronoun reference (the air of what — the door, the alcove?). "The last whole meal was close" is also vague — close in time? Close to being eaten? Close at hand?
**Why it weakens:** Ambiguity and repetition in a passage that wants menace.
**Suggested revision:** "He ate that evening. The alcove was nearly empty. The last whole meal was near. He opened the alcove door and stood in its cold for a moment, and the cold was not only cold."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 13
**Quote:** "He was banking the fire for sleep — pushing the coals together, laying a larger piece of wood across them to burn slow through the dark hours."
**Problem:** Weak progressive opener ("was banking"). The present-progressive frame is the weakest tense available for an action paragraph.
**Why it weakens:** Drains the gesture.
**Suggested revision:** "He banked the fire for sleep — pushed the coals together, laid a larger piece of wood across them to burn slow through the dark hours."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 14
**Quote:** "He was setting the second piece when he knew, with a sureness he had not built, that it should be at the back — angled so the draft fed the base, not the top."
**Problem:** Same weak progressive ("was setting").
**Why it weakens:** Same as above; back-to-back use compounds the limpness.
**Suggested revision:** "He set the second piece, and as he set it he knew, with a sureness he had not built, that it should be at the back — angled so the draft fed the base, not the top."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 15
**Quote:** "He adjusted the wood. He angled it toward the back of the firebox and positioned it so the gap beneath caught the draft from the gap in the wall."
**Problem:** "Angled" and "positioned" are doing duplicate work; "the gap beneath caught the draft from the gap in the wall" repeats "gap" awkwardly and reads as a diagram more than a sentence.
**Why it weakens:** Mechanical, over-described, breaks the chapter's rhythm.
**Suggested revision:** "He adjusted the wood. He angled it toward the back of the firebox so the draft from the wall could feed beneath."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 16
**Quote:** "It was the kind of thing he could have figured out himself. He had built fires every day. // He told himself this. The telling was adequate."
**Problem:** "The telling was adequate" / later "The telling held. Mostly." / earlier "The explanation held." The pattern of "X-ing held / was adequate" is repeated three times in close succession. By the third the device has become a tic.
**Why it weakens:** Recurring construction draws attention to the trick instead of the meaning. One use is striking; three is mannerism.
**Suggested revision:** Vary the second instance. E.g., replace "The explanation held." with "He let the explanation stand." or cut it entirely (the paragraph above already establishes the rationalization).
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 17
**Quote:** "More corrections followed. They came over the next days in the same register — terse, tactical, practical. He found himself moving the water pot to the left side of the hearth where the stone retained heat longer, with the same sense of prior knowledge — the conclusion arriving without the usual groping toward it, pre-assembled, confident of its own correctness."
**Problem:** Run-on / over-stuffed sentence. Three em-dash clauses ("with the same sense of prior knowledge — the conclusion arriving without the usual groping toward it, pre-assembled, confident of its own correctness") pile up after an already long sentence.
**Why it weakens:** The reader loses the spine of the sentence by the time the final qualifier lands.
**Suggested revision:** Break it: "He found himself moving the water pot to the left side of the hearth where the stone retained heat longer. The same sense of prior knowledge. The conclusion arrived pre-assembled, confident of its own correctness, without the usual groping toward it."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 18
**Quote:** "He checked the roof beam over the door not because he had noticed anything wrong with it but because the checking arrived in him like a remembered obligation."
**Problem:** Slightly wordy. "Not because...but because" works but the sentence around it is loose.
**Why it weakens:** Minor — the construction is intentional, but "had noticed anything wrong with it" is flabby.
**Suggested revision:** "He checked the roof beam over the door — not because anything looked wrong, but because the checking arrived like a remembered obligation."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 19
**Quote:** "Sleep facing the door. That one arrived at night — a flat imperative, the justification following a moment later as if appended, as if he had needed to think through what someone else already knew. The reasoning was so basic he should have known it already."
**Problem:** Two "as if" clauses in a row, then a sentence that restates the same idea ("he should have known it already") that the previous sentence ("as if he had needed to think through what someone else already knew") just made. Repetition.
**Why it weakens:** The third sentence is the second sentence's echo. One should go.
**Suggested revision:** Cut "The reasoning was so basic he should have known it already." Or cut the "as if he had needed to think through what someone else already knew" clause and keep the closer.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 20
**Quote:** "He was not hearing a voice. He was hearing himself, changed."
**Problem:** Weak progressive ("was hearing"). Could be sharper.
**Why it weakens:** Limp tense for a beat that wants to land.
**Suggested revision:** "He did not hear a voice. He heard himself, changed."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 21
**Quote:** "The last night of the chapter of his life that he would later think of as the cabin winter, he lay on the platform with the fire banked properly, the draft feeding the base, the heat directed and steady."
**Problem:** "The chapter of his life" is metafictional (the actual chapter is named Chapter 15) and clunky as a phrase. "Chapter of his life" is also a borderline cliche.
**Why it weakens:** Phrase calls attention to itself in a way that breaks immersion. The framing can be cleaner.
**Suggested revision:** "On the last night of what he would later think of as the cabin winter, he lay on the platform with the fire banked properly, the draft feeding the base, the heat directed and steady."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 22
**Quote:** "He lay in the dark and felt the silence and the silence felt like watching."
**Problem:** Triple "and" rhythm and the reuse of "felt" twice — once as physical sensation, once as metaphor — collapses the line. Also a borderline mixed image: silence cannot literally watch.
**Why it weakens:** The closing image deserves a sharper construction; the doubled "felt" weakens it.
**Suggested revision:** "He lay in the dark and felt the silence, and the silence felt like being watched." Or: "He lay in the dark. The silence felt like being watched."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 23
**Quote:** "And the silence continued its work — not quite empty."
**Problem:** "Continued its work" is slightly vague — the reader has to do an extra step to understand what "work" means here. The dash-tail "not quite empty" is fine.
**Why it weakens:** Mild abstraction in the final beat.
**Suggested revision:** "And the silence kept its watch — not quite empty." Or leave as is; the abstraction is doing intentional work.
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 24
**Quote:** "He went back inside. He built up the fire. He sat on the platform and stared at the flames. The looking would come later."
**Problem:** "The looking would come later" is too abstract — what looking? Looking at what? It feels like a placeholder for an idea that did not survive the draft.
**Why it weakens:** Reader has to guess at the referent. "Looking" is being used as a substitute for "thinking it through" or "reckoning" but the line does not say so.
**Suggested revision:** "He went back inside. He built up the fire. He sat on the platform and stared at the flames. The reckoning would come later."
**Priority:** Medium

## Overall Diagnosis

A strong, controlled chapter doing genuine literary work. The voice is intact, the Bleed escalation is well-paced, and the restrained horror lands. The main weak-writing problems are at the line level: a recurring weak progressive ("was banking," "was hearing," "was occupying"), a few sentences that say the same point two or three times before moving on, two sentences over-stuffed with em-dash clauses, and a small handful of vague closers ("the looking would come later," "the chapter of his life") where the abstraction does not earn its place. None of these break the chapter; together they soften it about ten percent below its ceiling.

## Top 10 Problems by Impact

1. **Issue 9** — Triple-restating that the strength is unnatural before delivering the new observation. (High)
2. **Issue 12** — Repeated "cold air," ambiguous pronoun, vague "last whole meal was close." (High)
3. **Issue 17** — Over-stuffed run-on with three em-dash clauses; loses spine. (High)
4. **Issue 21** — "The chapter of his life" — metafictional clunk and borderline cliche. (High)
5. **Issue 16** — "Telling/explanation held" repeated three times; mannerism. (Medium)
6. **Issue 13/14** — Back-to-back weak progressives ("was banking," "was setting"). (Medium)
7. **Issue 24** — "The looking would come later" — abstract placeholder. (Medium)
8. **Issue 19** — Repetition between "as if he had needed to think through" and "the reasoning was so basic." (Medium)
9. **Issue 4** — "His body was occupying the space differently" — weak progressive on a key beat. (Medium)
10. **Issue 22** — Doubled "felt" collapses the closing image. (Medium)

## Pattern Summary

Three recurring weaknesses:

1. **Weak progressive tense.** "Was banking," "was setting," "was hearing," "was occupying," "was making" — each instance drains the verb. The chapter's strongest sentences use simple past; the soft ones drift into progressive.
2. **Triple-saying.** Several paragraphs make the same observation in three consecutive constructions before moving on. The chapter's confident moments make the point once.
3. **Mannered echo.** "The telling was adequate" / "The telling held. Mostly." / "The explanation held." is striking the first time and a tic by the third. The trick should appear at most twice.

A minor fourth pattern: vague abstract closers ("the looking would come later," "the chapter of his life," "the silence continued its work") where a more concrete word would land harder.

## Revision Plan (Priority Order)

1. **High first:** Fix Issues 9, 12, 17, 21. These are the four moments where the prose stalls hardest.
2. **Then progressive-tense sweep:** Issues 4, 13, 14, 20. Convert "was X-ing" to simple past wherever the action is meant to land.
3. **Then repetition pruning:** Issues 16, 19. Remove or vary the third occurrence of "telling/explanation held" and cut the redundant sentence in the "sleep facing the door" paragraph.
4. **Then placeholder concretization:** Issues 24, 8. Replace "the looking" with a concrete word; trim the over-explained "testing" sentence.
5. **Then line-level tightening:** Issues 1, 2, 5, 6, 15, 22. Surgical cuts and re-orderings to remove wordiness without changing rhythm.
6. **Leave low-priority Issues 7, 10, 11, 18, 23 unless they fall out naturally** during the higher-priority pass.

The chapter does not need restructuring or scene-level work. The fixes are line-level only. Preserve every sentence whose weight is intentional — especially the unmarked Bleed register, the dark-humor pacing, and the closing image.
