# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 27: What the Hunter Eats

## Per-Issue Entries

### 1. Redundant echo of "carried"
- Quote: "the mineral quality that demon muscle carried regardless of the kill"
- Problem: "carried" is reused heavily in this chapter (tracks carried quality, he carried, tissues carried). Here it blurs with later uses.
- Revision: "the mineral quality demon muscle held regardless of the kill."
- Priority: Medium

### 2. Throat-clearing stacked sentence
- Quote: "She finished the strip and packed her kit and checked the trail."
- Problem: Three sequential ands in a short transitional beat flatten rhythm and read as filler.
- Revision: "She finished the strip, packed her kit, and checked the trail." — or split.
- Priority: Low

### 3. Over-explanation of maintenance
- Quote: "The benefits of consumption degraded over time if not sustained through regular intake. Strength declined over months. Sensory enhancement faded. The cold tolerance that let her work the mountain ranges receded if she went too long between meals."
- Problem: Four sentences saying the same thing: maintenance required. The list is exhaustive where one concrete image would cut deeper.
- Revision: Compress to two sentences — the principle, then one concrete loss.
- Priority: Medium

### 4. Telling the quality instead of showing it
- Quote: "The fieldcraft was still good. Still better than it should have been."
- Problem: Abstract assessment repeated. "Still... Still..." is throat-clearing.
- Revision: Merge: "The fieldcraft was still better than it should have been —"
- Priority: Medium

### 5. Abstract summation of pattern
- Quote: "What interested her now was the change in his pattern: the avoidance giving way to something more deliberate."
- Problem: "change in his pattern" + "avoidance giving way to something more deliberate" says the same thing twice in one sentence.
- Revision: "His avoidance was giving way to something more deliberate."
- Priority: Medium

### 6. Ladder exposition
- Quote: Paragraph beginning "She knew where she fit. At the bottom were the trappers..."
- Problem: Full hierarchy lecture inside a tracking scene. Slows momentum; reader likely knows the economy by ch27. Some compression warranted but the ladder itself is thematic — keep core, trim fat.
- Revision: Trim the middle tiers; keep trappers → hunters → patrons.
- Priority: Medium

### 7. Explained emotion / abstract philosophy
- Quote: "Evidence of an appetite that had become philosophy — the belief that flesh, properly consumed, could produce not merely strength but transformation."
- Problem: Narration interprets rather than renders. "Appetite that had become philosophy" is the kind of abstract gloss the weak-writing rule targets.
- Revision: Keep the sentence shorter and more concrete, or cut the gloss half.
- Priority: Medium

### 8. Weak connective throat-clearing
- Quote: "The morning warmed. She moved through the forest — efficient, low, staying off the ridge lines where her silhouette would carry."
- Problem: "The morning warmed" is a filler opener.
- Revision: Fold into next sentence or cut.
- Priority: Low

### 9. Repetition of "unusually"
- Quote: "unusually resilient, unusually fast"
- Problem: Adverb repetition where a single qualifier would do.
- Revision: "unusually resilient and fast"
- Priority: Low

### 10. Redundant "standard channels"
- Quote: "could not be replicated through the standard channels, because the standard channels did not include killing your brother..."
- Problem: Echoed phrase within one sentence; the second instance is the weak half.
- Revision: "...could not be replicated through the standard channels, which did not include killing your brother..."
- Priority: Medium

### 11. Wordy repetition
- Quote: "She had consumed demon flesh for years, and the consumption had never produced anything like what the witnesses described"
- Problem: "consumed... consumption" echo inside a single sentence.
- Revision: "She had eaten demon flesh for years, and it had never produced anything like what the witnesses described"
- Priority: Medium

### 12. Abstract closing of paragraph
- Quote: "The curiosity passed. She packed her kit and looked east along the trail."
- Problem: "The curiosity passed" tells an internal beat flatly; also she already packed her kit earlier in the chapter.
- Revision: Cut "The curiosity passed." Keep the action.
- Priority: Medium

### 13. Over-hedged speculation
- Quote: "It could mean he was planning. It could mean he was despairing. Either way, his pattern was shifting."
- Problem: Parallel speculation then summary is three beats where two would do.
- Revision: "It could mean planning. It could mean despair. His pattern was shifting either way."
- Priority: Low

### 14. Flat closing line
- Quote: "She was positioning. She was waiting."
- Problem: Two short parallel telling sentences flatten a strong chapter end. The sentence before already conveys positioning.
- Revision: Collapse to one — "She was positioning, and she was willing to wait."
- Priority: Medium

### 15. Wordy apothecary aside
- Quote: "rope and a soporific, something from the buyer's apothecary or prepared by herself"
- Problem: The "or prepared by herself" tail is unnecessary hedge.
- Revision: Cut "or prepared by herself."
- Priority: Low

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 27 is a measured, controlled interior-tracking chapter in Vark's POV. The prose holds register well and the voice is disciplined. Weak-writing issues cluster around two patterns: (1) mild over-explanation of the flesh economy and maintenance logic — saying the same thing in two or three sentences where one would cut deeper — and (2) small parallel-sentence tics ("She was X. She was Y.") that flatten ends of paragraphs. Nothing structural is broken; the chapter needs trimming, not rebuilding.

## Top 10 Problems Ranked

1. Over-explanation of maintenance (Issue 3)
2. Ladder exposition mid-scene (Issue 6)
3. Abstract "appetite became philosophy" gloss (Issue 7)
4. Flat closing "She was positioning. She was waiting." (Issue 14)
5. "standard channels" echo (Issue 10)
6. "consumed / consumption" echo (Issue 11)
7. "change in his pattern" redundancy (Issue 5)
8. "Still good. Still better." throat-clearing (Issue 4)
9. "The curiosity passed" telling (Issue 12)
10. "carried" overuse (Issue 1)

## Pattern Summary

Recurring weaknesses: (a) doubled statement — the idea said once in abstract and once in detail; (b) short parallel telling sentences at paragraph ends; (c) mild echoed vocabulary ("carried," "consumption," "standard channels") within close proximity; (d) occasional narrator gloss where the scene could stand alone.

## Revision Plan (Priority Order)

1. Compress the maintenance paragraph (Issue 3).
2. Trim the flesh-economy ladder to its essential tiers (Issue 6).
3. Cut or tighten the "appetite became philosophy" abstraction (Issue 7).
4. Rewrite the closing two sentences (Issue 14).
5. De-echo "standard channels" and "consumption" (Issues 10, 11).
6. Fix smaller redundancies: "change in pattern," "Still... Still...," "carried," "The curiosity passed," "unusually" (Issues 1, 4, 5, 9, 12).
7. Trim minor hedges and filler openers (Issues 2, 8, 13, 15).
