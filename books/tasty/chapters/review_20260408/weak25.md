# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 25: Two Sets of Eyes

## Per-Issue Findings

### 1. Redundant self-explanation (High)
Quote: "He lay on the ground where he had slept — no fire, a cold camp, the spring night mild enough that the blanket was sufficient — and waited for the presence to settle."
Problem: "on the ground where he had slept" is self-evident; the parenthetical piles three ways of saying the same thing (no fire / cold camp / mild night).
Fix: Trim to one concrete detail. "He lay in the cold camp and waited for the presence to settle."

### 2. Abstract telling of a concrete state (High)
Quote: "He moved through the day with the new awareness that the reconciliation of the previous days had produced. Not peace. Proximity — a closeness to the presence inside him that he had stopped fighting and had not yet accepted."
Problem: "awareness that the reconciliation... had produced" is an essay sentence. Abstract nouns stacked (awareness, reconciliation, proximity, closeness).
Fix: Cut the first sentence; let the paragraph-fragment "Not peace. Proximity." do the work.

### 3. Overexplained metaphor (High)
Quote: "The voice was the dream. A way of expressing what could not be compressed into terse corrections."
Problem: The second sentence re-states the first and tells the reader how to read it.
Fix: Delete the second sentence.

### 4. Repetition of the same point (High)
Quote: "Each morning now the boundary between his thoughts and the other thoughts took longer to reestablish, and each day the border was less clear."
Problem: "Each morning... / each day..." repeats the same observation twice with synonyms (boundary/border).
Fix: "Each morning the border took longer to reestablish, and held less."

### 5. Throat-clearing / dulling qualifier (Medium)
Quote: "The privacy of his own mind was no longer entirely private."
Problem: "entirely" softens the image and reads as a tidy summary line.
Fix: "The privacy of his own mind was no longer private."

### 6. Wordy construction (Medium)
Quote: "He thought about the dream's purpose — the fixed point the dreaming mind had been moving toward."
Problem: "the dreaming mind had been moving toward" is abstract and hedged.
Fix: "He thought about the dream's purpose — the fixed point it had been moving toward."

### 7. Redundant pair (Medium)
Quote: "public, even if the only audience was the trees and the road and the spring air."
Problem: Fine rhythm, but "public" is then glossed. Acceptable; Low.

### 8. Abstract summary of feeling (High)
Quote: "He was something harder than peace or healing or resolution: he was carrying his brother with open eyes. Love without clearing the debt. Love without making it smaller than it was."
Problem: The first sentence explains the emotional state; the fragments then re-state it twice. Tells the reader the theme.
Fix: Keep one line. "He was carrying his brother with open eyes."

### 9. Tell-then-explain (High)
Quote: "The person inside him was exactly what he had always been, and what he had always been could not be held in a single judgment."
Problem: Abstract thematic gloss. Says nothing the prior paragraph hasn't rendered.
Fix: Delete.

### 10. Weak closer explanation (Medium)
Quote: "two beings in one body, one alive and one dead, both real."
Problem: Labels what the chapter has already shown. Underlines the theme.
Fix: Delete the appositive; keep "the presence walked with him."

### 11. Filler sentence (Medium)
Quote: "The thought circled closer this time and almost touched the thing it was circling."
Problem: "circled... circling" repeats; "the thing it was circling" is a placeholder.
Fix: "The thought circled closer this time and almost touched."

### 12. Vague closer (Low)
Quote: "The feeling was a seed."
Problem: Cliche-adjacent but earned by the restraint around it. Keep.

### 13. Overwriting (Medium)
Quote: "He saw the tension in the woman's shoulders ease when he kept his distance. He saw her hand move from the knife at her belt to the cloth over the bread."
Problem: Two "He saw" openings in a row — similar openings, monotone rhythm.
Fix: Vary the second opening.

### 14. Redundant phrase (Medium)
Quote: "He said the name once. Quietly, without inflection — acknowledgment, not address."
Problem: "acknowledgment, not address" is a gloss that explains the tone already conveyed.
Fix: Delete the gloss.

### 15. Wordy ordering (Low)
Quote: "the first food in weeks that tasted of anything at all."
Fine. Keep.

## Overall Diagnosis
Strong restrained prose in the McCarthy register, but the chapter repeatedly stops to explain its own emotional arithmetic. The weak points are abstract summary sentences that paraphrase the image just rendered, and fragment-pairs that re-state a line that already landed. Cut the glosses and the chapter tightens without losing a beat.

## Top 10 Ranked
1. Abstract theme-telling at the climax ("something harder than peace...")
2. "The person inside him was exactly what he had always been..." gloss
3. Closer appositive ("two beings in one body, one alive and one dead, both real")
4. "The voice was the dream. A way of expressing..."
5. "He moved through the day with the new awareness..."
6. "Each morning... each day..." duplication
7. Opening cold-camp pileup
8. "entirely private" qualifier
9. "He saw... He saw..." repeated opening
10. "acknowledgment, not address" gloss

## Pattern Summary
The writer trusts the image, then distrusts it one sentence later. The recurring weakness is a gloss-sentence — usually abstract, often fragment-form — that paraphrases the rendered moment. Secondary pattern: doubled phrasings inside a single sentence (boundary/border, circled/circling, awareness/reconciliation).

## Revision Plan
1. Delete theme-glosses at paragraph ends (items 8, 9, 10).
2. Collapse duplicated pairs (items 3, 4, 11).
3. Tighten opening paragraph (item 1).
4. Cut abstract framing of "reconciliation" (item 2).
5. Trim qualifier and vary repeated openings (items 5, 13).
6. Drop "acknowledgment, not address" gloss (item 14).
