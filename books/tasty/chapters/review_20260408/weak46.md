# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 46: What He Carried

## Diagnosis

Chapter 46 is a quiet, reflective coda — almost entirely interior. The prose is controlled and intentional, and most of its "weaknesses" are in fact load-bearing stylistic choices (abstract grief language, anaphoric repetition, summary over scene). The genuine problems are narrow: a few abstractions where a concrete image would land harder, one or two sentences that overexplain what the structure has already delivered, and a small handful of throat-clearing or hedging phrases ("perhaps," "the kind of") that soften moments that should be exact.

## Issues

### 1. Abstract metaphor where concrete would land harder — Medium
**Quote:** "What surfaced was Hakiia as a permanent feature of the landscape of his mind, the way a mountain shapes the weather of a valley."
**Problem:** The mountain/valley simile is the kind of writerly comparison the rest of the chapter avoids. It explains the integration rather than enacts it.
**Fix:** Cut the simile; let the next three sentences (instincts, memories, preferences) do the work.

### 2. Overexplained abstraction — Medium
**Quote:** "His preferences were woven into the way Hurkuzak moved through the world, inseparable from his own."
**Problem:** "Inseparable from his own" restates "woven into." Redundant.
**Fix:** End the sentence at "the way Hurkuzak moved through the world."

### 3. Throat-clearing hedge — Low
**Quote:** "He was perhaps eight."
**Problem:** "Perhaps" softens. The narrator inhabits inherited memory; vagueness undercuts the claim of inheritance.
**Fix:** "He was eight."

### 4. Same hedge, second instance — Low
**Quote:** "Older now — perhaps fourteen."
**Fix:** "Older now. Fourteen."

### 5. Filler qualifier — Low
**Quote:** "the kind of territorial aggression that happened between young males in any community"
**Problem:** "in any community" is sociological throat-clearing — modern register and unnecessary.
**Fix:** Cut "in any community."

### 6. Weak hedging on duration — Low
**Quote:** "The watching had lasted perhaps a minute. Perhaps two."
**Problem:** Doubled "perhaps" calls attention to itself and weakens the line.
**Fix:** "The watching had lasted a minute. Maybe two."

### 7. Abstract summary clause — Medium
**Quote:** "the kind of aimless creation that children practice when they are alone with their hands and a medium"
**Problem:** "Children… alone with their hands and a medium" is abstract and slightly clinical ("a medium"). Pulls out of the moment.
**Fix:** Cut to: "patterns, shapes, the aimless drawing of a child alone."

### 8. Restated thesis — Medium
**Quote:** "Neither one brother nor the other. The third thing their collision had produced."
**Problem:** This is the chapter's thesis stated bluntly and very early. The chapter then re-earns it across four pages. Stating it here flattens the discovery.
**Fix:** Cut both sentences; let the closing pages arrive at the thought without prefiguring it.

### 9. Telling instead of letting the line stand — Medium
**Quote:** "He had not been allowed to feel everything at once — danger had kept the grief in pieces. He felt everything now,"
**Problem:** The first sentence explains the second. The structural shift to grief is already obvious from context.
**Fix:** Cut the first sentence; begin with "He felt everything now."

### 10. Sentence sprawl with redundant clause — Low
**Quote:** "and the not-living was a consequence of the not-learning, and the not-learning was a choice."
**Problem:** This is intentional rhetorical chaining (Hakiia's voice). Keep — flagged only to confirm it is a deliberate choice, not weakness.
**Fix:** None.

### 11. Empty intensifier / abstraction — Low
**Quote:** "the relentless campaign to harden a brother who did not want to be hardened"
**Problem:** "Relentless campaign" is mildly clichéd; "campaign" is faintly modern/political. Borderline given the chapter's register.
**Fix:** Acceptable — leave as-is.

### 12. Slight redundancy — Low
**Quote:** "The road continued east through lowland forest, unremarkable, ordinary."
**Problem:** "Unremarkable, ordinary" doubles the same beat.
**Fix:** Drop "unremarkable,".

## Top 10 Issues, Ranked

1. (#8) Premature statement of the chapter's thesis ("the third thing")
2. (#9) Explaining the grief shift before letting it land
3. (#1) Mountain/valley simile — abstract where concrete is needed
4. (#7) "Alone with their hands and a medium" — clinical abstraction
5. (#2) Redundant "inseparable from his own"
6. (#6) Doubled "perhaps" on duration
7. (#3) "Perhaps eight" hedge
8. (#4) "Perhaps fourteen" hedge
9. (#5) "in any community" sociological filler
10. (#12) "Unremarkable, ordinary" doublet

## Pattern Summary

Two recurring weaknesses: (a) abstraction-where-concrete-would-be-stronger, mostly via similes or generic noun phrases ("a medium," "any community," "the landscape of his mind"); (b) the narrator occasionally explains the chapter's own structural moves a beat before they happen, blunting them. Hedging "perhaps" appears three times in close proximity and forms a small tic.

## Revision Plan

1. Cut the early thesis statement so the closing pages can earn it.
2. Cut the explanatory sentence preceding "He felt everything now."
3. Cut the mountain/valley simile and the redundant "inseparable" clause.
4. Tighten the ash-drawing description.
5. Resolve the three "perhaps" hedges.
6. Remove "in any community" and "unremarkable,".

## Note on Failure Mode

Not a failure mode — a strong chapter with surface filings. Issues are isolated phrasings, not structural or voice problems.
