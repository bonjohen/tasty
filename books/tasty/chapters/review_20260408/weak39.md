# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 39: Neither and Both

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 39 is the integration beat: argument, memory, recognition, choice. The prose is largely controlled and the abstraction is mostly load-bearing for a chapter about identity dissolution. The main weaknesses are (1) hedging filler in a few key sentences, (2) one explicit "Not surrender. Recognition." gloss that explains what the surrounding paragraph already shows, (3) some redundant restating of "the love / the cruelty / the root" once the metaphor has landed, and (4) a couple of throat-clearing openers ("The argument continued through the night.", "He spoke at full range —"). None of these are register problems — the abstraction matches the McCarthy/Morrison-leaning voice — but a handful of lines do say the same thing twice.

---

## Issues

### 1. "He spoke at full range — with the fluency of a person who had been waiting a long time and who understood that the waiting was over."
- **Problem:** Wordy throat-clearing. "Full range" + "fluency" + "waiting was over" all push the same idea.
- **Why:** Three restatements of one beat blunt the entrance of Hakiia's monologue.
- **Revision:** Compress to one image. "He spoke with the fluency of someone who had been waiting a long time and knew the waiting was over."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 2. "perhaps twelve" / "perhaps four"
- **Problem:** Hedging qualifier in a memory rendered from inside Hakiia.
- **Why:** "Perhaps" softens what should be exact; the memory is his.
- **Revision:** Drop "perhaps."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 3. "the dim was comforting in the way that dim rooms are comforting to children because the dim reduces the world to a manageable size."
- **Problem:** Repetition of "dim" three times in one sentence; explanatory tail.
- **Why:** The triple "dim" is not rhythmic, it is stuck. The "because" clause explains an image the line already conveys.
- **Revision:** "The dimness was the kind that comforts children, reducing the world to a manageable size."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 4. "the particular defenseless sprawl that children achieve when they trust the space around them completely."
- **Problem:** "Particular" + "completely" are filler intensifiers.
- **Why:** Both can drop without losing meaning.
- **Revision:** "the defenseless sprawl of a child who trusts the space around him."
- **Priority:** Low

### 5. "Not surrender. Recognition."
- **Problem:** Narrator gloss naming the emotion before the paragraph renders it.
- **Why:** The next sentences already show recognition. The label tells the reader how to feel a beat that should arrive on its own.
- **Revision:** Cut the two-word gloss; let "He stopped trying to separate which thoughts were his..." carry it.
- **Priority:** High

### 6. "The instinct to protect — whose? Hakiia's instinct expressed through Hurkuzak's choice. The capacity for violence — Hakiia's capability wielded by Hurkuzak's judgment."
- **Problem:** Parallel construction repeated twice in a row, then a third time with grief.
- **Why:** The pattern is strong once. Repeated, it becomes a list. The grief sentence is the one that lands; the first two are scaffolding.
- **Revision:** Tighten — keep one of the two parallels, then move to grief.
- **Priority:** Medium

### 7. "But the using was his. The choosing was his."
- **Problem:** Two sentences saying one thing.
- **Why:** "The using" and "the choosing" are the same claim restated.
- **Revision:** "But the using was his."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 8. "the shaking that had been present since the capture, the shaking of a body in conflict with itself."
- **Problem:** "Shaking" repeated; the appositive over-explains.
- **Why:** The reader already knows why he is shaking.
- **Revision:** "the shaking that had been in him since the capture."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 9. "He looked at the walls of the study and the walls were the same walls and the room was the same room, but the person looking at them knew exactly what he was."
- **Problem:** Triple "walls" / "room" repetition is mannered without earning it; "exactly" is filler.
- **Why:** The McCarthy-style repetition works when the rhythm is doing work; here it just stalls.
- **Revision:** "He looked at the walls of the study. The room was unchanged. The person looking at it was not."
- **Priority:** Medium

### 10. "The argument continued through the night."
- **Problem:** Throat-clearing opener that summarizes what the next paragraphs will dramatize.
- **Why:** The chapter would lose nothing if it began with Hakiia speaking.
- **Revision:** Cut, or fold into the next paragraph.
- **Priority:** Low

### 11. "The night deepened. The stronghold was quiet."
- **Problem:** Two flat atmospheric sentences in a row.
- **Why:** Not a real beat; mostly filler between the two long monologues.
- **Revision:** Combine into one: "The night deepened, the stronghold quiet."
- **Priority:** Low

### 12. "Not because it excused Hakiia. The cruelty was real. The plan was real. The word vessel was real."
- **Problem:** "Real" repeated three times in tight sequence.
- **Why:** The repetition is intentional and works rhythmically — load-bearing. (Flagging only to confirm: keep.)
- **Priority:** Low — no change.

### 13. "The memory did not excuse any of that. It explained."
- **Problem:** Slight redundancy with the "Not because it excused Hakiia" two paragraphs earlier.
- **Why:** The point has been made.
- **Revision:** Keep — the second statement is the verdict and earns its echo. No change.
- **Priority:** Low — no change.

## Pattern Summary

Two recurring tendencies: (a) restating a clean image one more time than necessary (the "dim" sentence, the "walls" sentence, "using/choosing"), and (b) one or two narrator labels that name the emotional beat the prose is already carrying ("Not surrender. Recognition."). Hedging qualifiers ("perhaps," "particular," "completely," "exactly") cluster in the memory section.

## Revision Plan

1. Cut "Not surrender. Recognition." — let the next sentence carry it.
2. Compress the Hakiia-speaks entrance.
3. De-duplicate "dim" and "walls" sentences.
4. Trim the parallel "instinct / capacity / grief" sequence to one parallel + the grief.
5. Collapse "using / choosing" to one line.
6. Trim "shaking" appositive.
7. Drop "perhaps," "particular," "completely," "exactly."
8. Optionally cut the opener line; fold "night deepened" with "stronghold was quiet."
