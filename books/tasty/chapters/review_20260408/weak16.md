This pass flags only prose that is weak by the rule's criteria. Deliberate stylistic choices (weighted sentences, restrained horror, unmarked Bleed, dark register) are preserved.

## Per-Issue Findings

### Issue 1
**Quote:** "The voice had continued."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing transition. A flat declarative paragraph that announces what the next paragraph will dramatize.
**Why it weakens:** It tells the reader what they are about to be shown. The next paragraph already establishes that the voice is more frequent and harder to dismiss. Cutting this single-sentence paragraph tightens the section and lets the rendering carry the point.
**Suggested revision:** Delete the sentence/paragraph entirely.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 2
**Quote:** "It was more frequent now and harder to dismiss."
**Problem:** Telling instead of showing; abstract summary used where the next sentences provide the concrete evidence.
**Why it weakens:** The clause restates what the following lines render scenically. It is a topic sentence the paragraph does not need.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the sentence; let "The corrections had graduated…" carry the opening.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 3
**Quote:** "But the voice had more to say."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing single-line paragraph. A pure transition sentence that announces, then steps aside.
**Why it weakens:** It exists only to bridge — adds no image, no pressure, no information. The book's voice prefers compressed weight over signposting.
**Suggested revision:** Delete the line.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 4
**Quote:** "Then the correction came."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing transition; a second signpost in the same beat as Issue 3.
**Why it weakens:** The correction itself is the next paragraph. Announcing it dilutes the moment of intrusion. The Bleed should arrive without warning.
**Suggested revision:** Delete the line. Let the correction sentence land cold.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 5
**Quote:** "He was sitting by the hearth on a day when the wind was quiet, and his mind drifted to the journey — the trail before the cliff, the path Hakiia had chosen along the ridgeline. He was thinking about the argument — the trees and the ridge, Hakiia's insistence on the exposed path."
**Problem:** Repetition of the same point in two consecutive sentences; "He was sitting / He was thinking" both lean on the weak progressive construction.
**Why it weakens:** The second sentence restates the first ("his mind drifted to the journey" → "He was thinking about the argument"). Two passes at the same idea, both built on "He was [verb]ing," produce monotone rhythm and slow the entry into the memory.
**Suggested revision:** Compress to one sentence. Replace progressive with active: "He sat by the hearth on a day when the wind was quiet, thinking about the argument — the trees and the ridge, Hakiia's insistence on the exposed path."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 6
**Quote:** "the old confusion of a man trying to understand why his brother chose speed over mercy."
**Problem:** Explaining emotion / interpreting the scene. The narrator names what the memory means rather than letting the memory carry it.
**Why it weakens:** The chapter has already established the dog and the ridge. The phrase "speed over mercy" is the writer telling the reader what to feel. This is the kind of summarizing CLAUDE.md flags.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the gloss. End at "his own thinking: hurt, bewildered." The texture words do the work.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 7
**Quote:** "He looked at the fire. He checked the door. He did not name what he had heard."
**Problem:** Acceptable rhythm in itself, but it sets up the next paragraph which immediately repeats "He did not name it." Repetition of the same point.
**Why it weakens:** "He did not name what he had heard" and the following "He did not name it" are the same beat, two sentences apart. One of them must go.
**Suggested revision:** Keep this line; rework the next paragraph's opening so it does not begin with the same phrase (see Issue 9).
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 8
**Quote:** "It had a particular way of waiting — the certainty of a person who does not need to argue because they already know which way the argument will resolve."
**Problem:** Slightly overwritten / explanatory. The em-dash clause is doing analytical work the next sentences already render.
**Why it weakens:** "Hurkuzak had been on the receiving end of that kind of patience before. He recognized it without wanting to" already delivers the meaning. The "certainty of a person who…" gloss is the writer underlining.
**Suggested revision:** Trim to: "It had a particular way of waiting." Let the next two sentences carry the rest.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 9
**Quote:** "He did not name it. To name it would be to say his dead brother's mind was alive inside his own, and he did not want to think the thing he was starting to think — because if he thought it, there was no way to unthink it. Because it might be true."
**Problem:** Overexplanation; the paragraph states the subtext directly. Also a run-on middle sentence with stacked because-clauses.
**Why it weakens:** The chapter has spent its prior beats refusing to name the thing. This paragraph then names it in plain narration ("his dead brother's mind was alive inside his own"). It collapses the very pressure the scene was building. Per CLAUDE.md: "summarizing emotion instead of rendering it."
**Suggested revision:** Compress and indirect. Something like: "To name it would be to make it true. He did not name it."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 10
**Quote:** "The voice had other opinions. It disliked the way Hurkuzak ate — slowly, carefully, with the deliberation of a person who was still thinking about each bite. There was impatience in it, the pressure of a person waiting for you to stop hesitating. One evening he pressed his hand against the granite wall behind the hearth and found it warm…"
**Problem:** Abrupt topic shift inside a single paragraph. The granite-wall observation is a different beat from the eating-impatience beat and belongs in its own paragraph.
**Why it weakens:** Two unrelated intrusions of the Bleed are mashed together. The reader loses the second one because the first is still resolving.
**Suggested revision:** Break paragraph at "One evening he pressed his hand…"
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 11
**Quote:** "the deliberation of a person who was still thinking about each bite"
**Problem:** Wordy; "who was still thinking about each bite" is a weak progressive clause that slows the line.
**Why it weakens:** "the deliberation of a person still weighing each bite" is tighter and stronger.
**Suggested revision:** "the deliberation of a person still weighing each bite."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 12
**Quote:** "The thought arrived, unbidden, that whoever built this cabin had known that. Had chosen the granite face for that reason. Had placed the hearth against it so the stone would work through the night after the fire burned low."
**Problem:** "Unbidden" is a cliche in this exact phrase ("the thought arrived unbidden"). Acceptable in moderation, but combined with the following three explanatory clauses it overstates.
**Why it weakens:** Cliche + a list of three "Had…" clauses that walks the reader through the logic. The cabin-was-built-with-intent point lands in one beat; three is over-explanation.
**Suggested revision:** "The thought arrived that whoever built this cabin had known. Had chosen the granite face for that reason." Drop the third clause.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 13
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak had never thought about a cabin this way."
**Problem:** Telling instead of showing; explanatory tag at the end of the paragraph.
**Why it weakens:** The whole paragraph already demonstrates that he is thinking about the cabin in a new way. Stating it flattens the moment.
**Suggested revision:** Delete the sentence.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 14
**Quote:** "But the softness Hakiia had tried to burn away was diminished. Not gone, but overlaid with something harder — a stillness that watched."
**Problem:** Mild — passive construction "had tried to burn away was diminished." Slightly weak. The image is good; the verb is limp.
**Why it weakens:** "was diminished" is a flat past-passive. An active formulation lands harder.
**Suggested revision:** "But the softness Hakiia had tried to burn away had thinned. Not gone, but overlaid with something harder — a stillness that watched."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 15
**Quote:** "Not permanently. Not as a departure. As a test."
**Problem:** Effective fragment cluster — keep as is. Flagged here only to note it was reviewed and judged intentional.
**Priority:** None (no change)

### Issue 16
**Quote:** "He could track. He had never learned to."
**Problem:** Effective. Reviewed and judged intentional.
**Priority:** None (no change)

### Issue 17
**Quote:** "the depression of the prints still faintly visible under a skin of new powder"
**Problem:** "faintly visible" is a weak adverb-plus-adjective construction; "still" adds little.
**Why it weakens:** "faintly visible" is the kind of soft phrasing the rule flags. The chapter elsewhere prefers concrete verbs.
**Suggested revision:** "the depression of the prints still showing under a skin of new powder."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 18
**Quote:** "He had used everything — bone for broth, marrow for sustenance, the final sinew boiled soft."
**Problem:** "marrow for sustenance" is abstract where the sentence is otherwise concrete.
**Why it weakens:** "Sustenance" is a soft, ledger-style noun next to "bone for broth" (concrete) and "the final sinew boiled soft" (concrete). It breaks the rhythm by being the only abstract item in the list.
**Suggested revision:** "bone for broth, marrow rendered, the final sinew boiled soft."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 19
**Quote:** "The days continued. The light grew. The snow compacted further. The first sounds of running water came from somewhere below the cabin — a stream or a seep released by the earliest loosening of ice."
**Problem:** "The days continued" is throat-clearing; "further" is filler; "the earliest loosening of ice" is wordy where "the first loosening" would be tighter.
**Why it weakens:** Three weak openers in one paragraph. The opening sentence has no image. "Compacted further" is a weaker version of "compacted" — the chapter has already established this twice.
**Suggested revision:** Cut "The days continued." Cut "further." Tighten "the earliest loosening of ice" to "the first loosening of ice."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 20
**Quote:** "The season was turning. The cabin's grip was loosening. Somewhere beyond the mountains was a world he would need to reenter."
**Problem:** "was turning" / "was loosening" — two consecutive progressive constructions; weaker than active equivalents. Also restates points the previous paragraph has just made (light growing, snow compacting, water running).
**Why it weakens:** Repetition of the same point. The previous paragraph already showed the season turning. This paragraph paraphrases.
**Suggested revision:** Compress to one sentence. "The cabin's grip was loosening. Beyond the mountains was a world he would need to reenter."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 21
**Quote:** "He could feel his own stillness — not the stillness of cold or exhaustion, but the stillness of a body that was waiting for something, listening for something, in the way a person listens when they know the other presence in the room is also listening."
**Problem:** Long sentence; the trailing simile ("in the way a person listens when they know the other presence in the room is also listening") explains what the prior clause has already shown.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence is doing strong work until the simile. The simile then re-explains. Trim it.
**Suggested revision:** "He could feel his own stillness — not the stillness of cold or exhaustion, but the stillness of a body waiting, listening, aware that the other presence in the room was listening too."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 22
**Quote:** "He had not asked for this. He had not consented to it. He had simply survived, and survival had its costs, and the costs did not stop accruing when the act was over."
**Problem:** "Costs accruing" is a slightly modern/ledger register; the abstract "survival had its costs, and the costs did not stop accruing" is the kind of summary line the book otherwise resists.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence explains the theme rather than rendering it. "Costs accruing" pulls toward bookkeeping vocabulary. The book's voice should land this without commentary.
**Suggested revision:** "He had not asked for this. He had not consented to it. He had survived, and survival did not end when the act ended."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 23
**Quote:** "He would leave soon. That was all he knew. He would walk out of these mountains carrying more than he had when he walked in, and not all of it was his."
**Problem:** "That was all he knew" is throat-clearing.
**Why it weakens:** It interrupts the closing rhythm. "He would leave soon" → "He would walk out of these mountains…" lands cleaner without the middle sentence.
**Suggested revision:** Cut "That was all he knew."
**Priority:** Medium

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 16 is doing intentional, atmospheric work — the cabin thaw, the second presence settling in, Hurkuzak's recognition that he is no longer only himself. The weighted sentences and the unmarked Bleed are working as designed. What weakens the chapter is a recurring habit of telling the reader what the scene already shows: short throat-clearing transition lines ("The voice had continued.", "But the voice had more to say.", "Then the correction came."), summary topic sentences placed in front of paragraphs that already render the same point, and one critical paragraph that names the subtext outright ("his dead brother's mind was alive inside his own") right after the chapter has spent four beats refusing to name it. The chapter's late paragraphs also repeat the seasonal-turning beat twice and slip toward abstract ledger-vocabulary ("costs accruing"). Trim the throat-clearing, compress the doubled beats, refuse to name what should stay unnamed, and the chapter tightens without losing any of its texture.

## Pattern Summary

Three recurring weaknesses run through the chapter:

1. **Throat-clearing and signposting.** Multiple single-line paragraphs and topic-sentence openers exist only to announce what the next paragraph will render. The book's voice does not need them. Cut, and the scene gets denser.
2. **Summary closers and interpretive tags.** Several paragraphs that work scenically end with a sentence that explains the meaning. ("the old confusion of a man trying to understand…", "Hurkuzak had never thought about a cabin this way.", "his dead brother's mind was alive inside his own.") This is the exact failure mode the book's CLAUDE.md warns against: summarizing emotion instead of rendering it.
3. **Repeated beats / progressive constructions.** "He was sitting / He was thinking", "The season was turning / The cabin's grip was loosening", "The days continued / The light grew", and the doubled "He did not name it" all deliver the same point twice with weaker, slower verb forms the second time.

A fourth, smaller pattern: a few abstract or ledger-register words ("sustenance," "costs accruing," "faintly visible") drift away from the chapter's otherwise concrete, physical vocabulary.
