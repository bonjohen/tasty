# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 33: The Unexpected Result (2026-04-08)

Reviewed against `.claude/rules/weak-writing.md`. Intentional stylistic features (unmarked Bleed voice, dense imagery, restrained horror, McCarthy-leaning rhythm) are preserved.

## Per-Issue Findings

### Issue 1 — Throat-clearing opening — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "He was sitting by a fire on the fourth night east of the ravine, the correspondence fragments spread before him, the ceramic containers standing in their row, and he was reading the second fragment again when a certainty arrived."
**Problem:** Two past-progressive verbs in one sentence drain momentum before the real beat (the certainty arriving).
**Fix:** "He sat by a fire on the fourth night east of the ravine, the correspondence fragments spread before him, the ceramic containers standing in their row, reading the second fragment again when a certainty arrived."

### Issue 2 — Repetition of "design" — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "The shape was the merchant prince's design... The design was not new. The design had a history..."
**Problem:** "Design" four times in four sentences crosses from incantation into mechanical repetition.
**Fix:** "It was not new. It had a history, long and specific..."

### Issue 3 — Wordy filler / second-person slip — LOW
**Quote:** "the way a map gives you the shape of a territory you have never visited"
**Fix:** "the way a map gives the shape of a territory never visited."

### Issue 4 — Redundant em-dash gloss — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "That was addition — what a man is when he takes on another's strength remains himself."
**Problem:** The gloss restates the prior clause.
**Fix:** "That was addition. The man taking on another's strength remained himself."

### Issue 5 — "Not merely" ladder — LOW
**Quote:** "Not merely strength but instinct. Not merely instinct but memory. Not merely memory but personality."
**Problem:** Symmetry borderline mannered. Keep — earned by escalation.

### Issue 6 — Verb pile-up / synonym stack — HIGH
**Quote:** "The failures came as a density of knowledge that Hurkuzak absorbed as a mass, the way a man absorbs the history of a war not as discrete events but as a weight."
**Problem:** "absorbed as a mass" / "absorbs the history" repeats the verb; "density / mass / weight" piles three near-synonyms.
**Fix:** "The failures came as a density of knowledge, the way a man takes in the history of a war not as discrete events but as a weight."

### Issue 7 — Vague and wordy — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "though the voice offered something and pulled back as though the offering was too close to a confession"
**Fix:** "though the voice offered, then pulled back, as if the offering lay too close to a confession."

### Issue 8 — Polysyndeton overload — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "The shape was enough, and the shape was Hakiia's, and it arrived without invitation, and it was too close to his own face to look at directly."
**Problem:** Four "and" clauses; same device runs in adjacent paragraphs.
**Fix:** "The shape was enough. It was Hakiia's, and it arrived without invitation, and it was too close to his own face to look at directly."

### Issue 9 — Clinical phrasing — HIGH
**Quote:** "the total emotional attachment of a brother who loved the man he was eating"
**Problem:** "total emotional attachment" is clinical abstraction in a passage that should land as image.
**Fix:** "the whole helpless love of a brother who loved the man he was eating"

### Issue 10 — Three glosses of one insight — HIGH
**Quote:** "The patron's ordered trials had failed because they were ordered. The precision was the poison. Cold method produced cold results — hollow transfers..."
**Problem:** Three consecutive sentences gloss the same idea before the concrete image.
**Fix:** Cut the middle gloss. "The patron's ordered trials had failed because they were ordered. Cold method produced cold results — hollow transfers..."

### Issue 11 — Faintly clinical verb — LOW
**Quote:** "The voice inside him was not degrading. It was settling."
**Fix:** "The voice inside him was not unraveling. It was settling."

### Issue 12 — Awkward apposition — LOW
**Quote:** "Something finding its place by the terrible intimacy..."
**Fix:** "Something finding its place through the terrible intimacy..."

### Issue 13 — Announcement before image — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "He held three truths without resolving them — the clarity of a man who sees all the walls of a room at once and cannot pretend the room is smaller than it is."
**Problem:** "He held three truths without resolving them" is throat-clearing before the strong image.
**Fix:** "Three truths, held without resolving — the clarity of a man who sees all the walls of a room at once and cannot pretend the room is smaller than it is."

### Issue 14 — Register slip — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "processed by a system that saw his body as an asset and his integration as a breakthrough"
**Problem:** "asset," "integration as a breakthrough" — corporate/clinical idiom drifting from pre-industrial register.
**Fix:** "weighed by a system that saw his body as a yield and his survival as a proof"

### Issue 15 — Filler echo — LOW
**Quote:** "He had been running east, always east, away from the thing he had done"
**Fix:** "He had been running east, away from the thing he had done"

### Issue 16 — Summary commentary — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "The machine did not know this. That unknowing was the first advantage he had held since the cabin."
**Problem:** Second sentence explains the moment.
**Fix:** "The machine did not know this. It was the first advantage he had held since the cabin."

### Issue 17 — Flat trailing tag — MEDIUM
**Quote:** "Two brothers in one body, and both wanting the reckoning — the first thing they had wanted together. It carried them east."
**Problem:** "It carried them east" is a small flat tag that shrinks the larger image.
**Fix:** "Two brothers in one body, both wanting the reckoning — the first thing they had wanted together, and it carried them east."

## Diagnosis

A confident chapter doing real work. Voice and register are intact and the structural beats land. The prose weakens in three predictable places: (1) summary sentences that gloss what the image already conveyed, (2) abstract or faintly clinical word choices ("asset," "integration," "total emotional attachment," "degrading") that drift from the pre-industrial register, and (3) repetition ("design," polysyndeton) that crosses from incantation into mannerism.

## Pattern Summary

- **Glossing**: Concrete sentences are followed by abstract sentences that explain them.
- **Clinical drift**: System-speak leaks into the narration when the chapter is talking about the system.
- **Mannerism creep**: Anaphora and polysyndeton recur within paragraphs of each other.
- **Weak tags after strong images**: Small flat sentences shrink larger images.
