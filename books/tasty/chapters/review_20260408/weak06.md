# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 6: What Hunger Teaches

Reviewed against `.claude/rules/weak-writing.md`. The chapter is in the book's intentional register (weighted, McCarthy-leaning, restrained horror, unmarked Bleed). Issues below are limited to prose that is genuinely weak by the rule's criteria — not stylistic features the book is committed to.

---

## Per-Issue Findings

### Issue 1
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak had been pulling the travois for what felt like hours but may have been less."
**Problem:** Wordy throat-clearing opener for the paragraph; the hedge ("what felt like hours but may have been less") is a flat way to express subjective time and undercuts itself.
**Why it weakens:** Two qualifiers fight each other and produce vagueness. The chapter has already opened with a clean line ("The first wolf came at dusk."); this sentence dilutes the momentum.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the hedge. "Hurkuzak had been pulling the travois for hours, or what felt like hours."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 2
**Quote:** "His ribs sent a flare of pain with every breath that went deeper than shallow."
**Problem:** "deeper than shallow" is awkward and abstract where concrete language is available.
**Why it weakens:** The phrase trips the ear and sounds like a workaround for "any breath he tried to take properly."
**Suggested revision:** "His ribs flared with every breath that wasn't shallow."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 3
**Quote:** "never close enough to count reliably but never far enough to forget."
**Problem:** The construction is fine but the adverb "reliably" is filler — it adds a hedge the sentence doesn't need.
**Why it weakens:** Cleaner without it; the parallelism lands harder.
**Suggested revision:** "never close enough to count, never far enough to forget."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 4
**Quote:** "It was large -- larger than the dogs had been, with a thick winter coat and a wide head and eyes that caught the failing light and gave none of it back."
**Problem:** "It was large" is a weak telling opener followed by the actual concrete detail. The throat-clearing is unnecessary because the comparison ("larger than the dogs") does the work.
**Why it weakens:** Telling before showing; the strong image at the end is muffled by an opener that says nothing.
**Suggested revision:** "Larger than the dogs had been, with a thick winter coat and a wide head and eyes that caught the failing light and gave none of it back."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 5
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak stopped pulling. He turned to face the wolf and his hand went to the hatchet on his belt and the wolf watched his hand move and did not retreat."
**Problem:** The polysyndeton is in-voice, but "his hand went to the hatchet" is a weak passive-construction substitute for an active verb.
**Why it weakens:** "His hand went to" is a tic that recurs in the chapter and softens action. He reaches.
**Suggested revision:** "He turned to face the wolf and reached for the hatchet on his belt and the wolf watched his hand move and did not retreat."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 6
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak dragged the travois to the rocks Hakiia had indicated -- a low cluster of boulders at the foot of the valley wall, large enough to block approach from one direction and create a corner where the wolves could not come from behind."
**Problem:** "Hakiia had indicated" is bureaucratic and weak; it labels the action instead of letting it ride from the previous dialogue. "Create a corner where the wolves could not come from behind" is a wordy explainer.
**Why it weakens:** The reader already knows Hakiia just pointed at the rocks. The clause restates it. The second half tells the reader the tactical purpose instead of trusting the geometry.
**Suggested revision:** "Hurkuzak dragged the travois to the rocks — a low cluster of boulders at the foot of the valley wall, big enough to block approach from one side and put stone behind Hakiia's back."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 7
**Quote:** "Hakiia held it the way he held all weapons -- low, angled, the blade an extension of his forearm rather than something gripped in his fist."
**Problem:** "rather than something gripped in his fist" is an unnecessary explanation that defines by negation what the prior phrase already shows.
**Why it weakens:** The image is already complete at "an extension of his forearm." The negation flattens it.
**Suggested revision:** "Hakiia held it the way he held all weapons — low, angled, the blade an extension of his forearm."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 8
**Quote:** "The wait was worse than the attack would be."
**Problem:** Telling-on-the-nose. The narration interprets the moment instead of letting the next paragraph (the circling, the closing distance) carry it.
**Why it weakens:** Pre-explains the dread the reader is about to feel. Cliché in shape.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the line entirely. The next paragraph already proves it.
**Priority:** High

### Issue 9
**Quote:** "drawn by the commotion or the blood or the simple gravity of opportunity."
**Problem:** "the simple gravity of opportunity" is overwritten — straining for effect, abstract where the previous two items are concrete.
**Why it weakens:** Mixed register inside a single triple. The first two items are physical; the third is a metaphor that calls attention to itself.
**Suggested revision:** "drawn by the commotion or the blood." (Or replace the third item with another concrete cue.)
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 10
**Quote:** "They spread themselves around the clearing in a loose formation that covered every approach, and they watched -- spread wide, none of them looking at each other, and yet the spacing was exact."
**Problem:** "spread themselves" then "spread wide" inside the same sentence — accidental repetition. "and yet" is a soft connector; the contrast is already implicit.
**Why it weakens:** The repeated verb is audible; the soft transition deflates the punch of "the spacing was exact."
**Suggested revision:** "They settled around the clearing in a loose formation that covered every approach, and they watched — none of them looking at each other, the spacing exact."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 11
**Quote:** "The hatchet caught air."
**Problem:** None — strong line. Noted only to anchor the surrounding analysis.
**Priority:** N/A

### Issue 12
**Quote:** "the impact jolted up through his arm and the wolf yelped and scrambled sideways and Hurkuzak followed it, swinging a third time, and the third swing went wide because his footing was wrong and his weight was forward and the hatchet buried itself in the snow instead of the animal."
**Problem:** Run-on. The polysyndeton works elsewhere because the clauses are short and physical; here the sentence accumulates causation ("because his footing was wrong and his weight was forward") that wants a stop.
**Why it weakens:** The momentum becomes blur. The miss should land harder.
**Suggested revision:** Break after "swinging a third time." "He swung a third time. The swing went wide — his footing wrong, his weight forward — and the hatchet buried itself in the snow."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 13
**Quote:** "and the pain was immediate and enormous and deeply wrong"
**Problem:** "deeply" is an empty intensifier; "enormous" is vague where the next clause does the concrete work.
**Why it weakens:** The intensifier-stack ("immediate and enormous and deeply wrong") tries to force a feeling the next sentence already earns through specificity ("the grinding pressure of jaws designed to hold and not let go").
**Suggested revision:** "and the pain was immediate and wrong" — or cut the abstract triple entirely and let the jaws-clause stand.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 14
**Quote:** "He screamed. He did not decide to scream. His body screamed because it had been seized by something that would not release it."
**Problem:** Three sentences saying the same thing with progressively more explanation. The third sentence is the one that explains; the first two were enough.
**Why it weakens:** Unnecessary repetition; the third sentence over-tells and softens the involuntary horror of the first two.
**Suggested revision:** "He screamed. He did not decide to scream."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 15
**Quote:** "He swung the hatchet with his free arm. The angle was bad and the force was worse but the blade struck bone -- skull or jaw, he couldn't tell -- and the wolf released and stumbled and Hurkuzak hit it again while it was still staggering and this time the hatchet sank into something that gave way and the wolf went down and did not get up."
**Problem:** Long polysyndeton run that wants one internal break. "The angle was bad and the force was worse" is also a slightly cute symmetry that sits oddly in the violence.
**Why it weakens:** Reader can lose the beat. A stop after "released and stumbled" would let the second blow land.
**Suggested revision:** Break the sentence after "stumbled." Begin the next: "Hurkuzak hit it again while it was still staggering, and this time the hatchet sank into something that gave way, and the wolf went down and did not get up."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 16
**Quote:** "wolves knew where damage lived -- not through thought but through something older and more reliable."
**Problem:** "not through thought but through something older and more reliable" is interpretive narration that flattens an otherwise strong image.
**Why it weakens:** Tells the reader what to feel about wolf instinct. The phrase "wolves knew where damage lived" already does the work.
**Suggested revision:** "wolves knew where damage lived."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 17
**Quote:** "Hakiia's upper body rose from the travois in a motion that should not have been possible for a man with two shattered legs and nothing left to burn."
**Problem:** "in a motion that should not have been possible for…" is narratorial commentary that explains the image instead of trusting it.
**Why it weakens:** Tells the reader the action is impossible. Showing the cost (the next two clauses about the shoulder) is already the proof.
**Suggested revision:** "Hakiia's upper body rose from the travois." (Let the cost-detail that follows do the rest.)
**Priority:** High

### Issue 18
**Quote:** "the sound Hakiia made was not a sound that came from a place where words existed."
**Problem:** Overwritten, straining for effect. The construction loops back on itself ("a sound… not a sound… from a place where words existed").
**Why it weakens:** Calls attention to its own poetic effort. A tighter phrasing hits harder.
**Suggested revision:** "the sound Hakiia made had no language in it."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 19
**Quote:** "The massive jaw that had been clamped around nothing during the dying convulsed one final time, a mechanical spasm that had nothing to do with life, and the jaw found Hakiia's right hand"
**Problem:** "that had nothing to do with life" is an explanatory tag that softens the horror; the word "mechanical" already carries the meaning.
**Why it weakens:** Doubles the point. The mechanical spasm is enough.
**Suggested revision:** "The massive jaw convulsed one final time, a mechanical spasm, and found Hakiia's right hand"
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 20
**Quote:** "with the blind grinding force of a dead thing that had not finished killing."
**Problem:** Strong line, but "blind grinding force" stacks two adjectives where one would do, and "that had not finished killing" is the actual idea.
**Why it weakens:** Slight overwriting. Trim the adjective stack.
**Suggested revision:** "with the grinding force of a dead thing that had not finished killing."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 21
**Quote:** "Hakiia made a sound. Not the compressed sound from before. Something involuntary and worse."
**Problem:** "Something involuntary and worse" is abstract telling where a concrete sound or beat would do more.
**Why it weakens:** The reader is told the sound is worse; the prose doesn't make it worse.
**Suggested revision:** Either let the prior fragment stand alone ("Hakiia made a sound. Not the compressed sound from before.") or replace the third fragment with a concrete cue.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 22
**Quote:** "He grabbed the wolf's muzzle and pried and the jaw resisted with the locked strength of a dead thing's last contraction and he pried harder and the jaw opened and Hakiia's hand came free and the hand was wrong."
**Problem:** Run-on; the cumulative polysyndeton blurs four distinct beats into one breath. The strong final clause ("the hand was wrong") gets buried.
**Why it weakens:** Diminishes the punch of the reveal that follows.
**Suggested revision:** Break before "and the hand was wrong" so the line stands. "He grabbed the wolf's muzzle and pried, and the jaw resisted with the locked strength of a dead thing's last contraction, and he pried harder until it opened. Hakiia's hand came free. The hand was wrong."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 23
**Quote:** "He could not stop it -- too many openings, each one pouring freely."
**Problem:** Restates the prior sentence ("It came from every opened surface, and the snow around Hakiia's hand turned dark in a spreading circle that grew too fast"). Also "pouring freely" is a soft phrase.
**Why it weakens:** The point has already been made. The added sentence is explanation after the image.
**Suggested revision:** Cut. The previous sentence already says it.
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 24
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak built the fire back up with hands that were shaking and fed it until the flames were high enough and held the knife blade in the center of the fire and watched the metal change color while his brother bled into the snow."
**Problem:** Run-on accumulating four actions into one breath; "high enough" is vague.
**Why it weakens:** The image of watching metal change color while a brother bleeds is the strong beat. The sentence buries it under the construction.
**Suggested revision:** "Hurkuzak built the fire back up with shaking hands, fed it until the flames were high, and held the knife blade in the center. He watched the metal change color while his brother bled into the snow."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 25
**Quote:** "The smell was rendered flesh, the sweetness of burning tissue."
**Problem:** Strong line. Noted only because "rendered flesh" lands well — keep.
**Priority:** N/A

### Issue 26
**Quote:** "The exertion of the wolf kill -- the shoulder forced past what the cliff damage had left intact, the grinding Hurkuzak had heard, the weight of the wolf held by a joint that was already failing -- had completed what the fall had started."
**Problem:** Long parenthetical chain inside a sentence whose final clause ("had completed what the fall had started") is also abstract. The interruption explains rather than shows.
**Why it weakens:** The prior paragraph already showed the shoulder grinding under the wolf's weight. This sentence catalogs what the reader watched.
**Suggested revision:** "The exertion of the wolf kill had finished what the fall began."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 27
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak watched Hakiia try to lift it once, his face concentrated with the effort, and the arm did not respond. The fingers did not twitch."
**Problem:** "his face concentrated with the effort" is filler that restates the implied action; "did not respond" is bureaucratic.
**Why it weakens:** Two slightly weak phrases bracket the moment. The fingers-line is good; the rest can be trimmed.
**Suggested revision:** "Hurkuzak watched Hakiia try to lift it once. The arm did not move. The fingers did not twitch."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 28
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak stood in the bloody snow with the hatchet shaking in his hand and his forearm bleeding and his body vibrating with something that was not shaking and not stillness and not anything he recognized."
**Problem:** The triple negation ("not shaking and not stillness and not anything he recognized") strains for effect and ends in a vague abstraction.
**Why it weakens:** Defining by negation works once; three times it becomes a tic and the final phrase ("anything he recognized") is the kind of vague summary the rule flags.
**Suggested revision:** "Hurkuzak stood in the bloody snow with the hatchet shaking in his hand and his forearm bleeding and his body vibrating with something he had no name for."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 29
**Quote:** "The silence after was not peace. It was the temporary absence of violence."
**Problem:** "the temporary absence of violence" is abstract clinical phrasing that drifts toward the modern register the book avoids.
**Why it weakens:** Tells the reader what the silence isn't, in a register slightly out of voice.
**Suggested revision:** "The silence after was not peace. It was the pause between killings."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 30
**Quote:** "There was a slackening in him that did not look like rest, a settling that did not look like ease."
**Problem:** Twin parallel clauses both built on negation ("did not look like X… did not look like Y"). Slightly mannered.
**Why it weakens:** The doubling is decorative rather than additive. One would do.
**Suggested revision:** "There was a slackening in him that did not look like rest."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 31
**Quote:** "His right hand could not hold it. His right hand could not hold anything."
**Problem:** Intentional anaphora — keep. Noted because it could be misread as repetition; it's working.
**Priority:** N/A

### Issue 32
**Quote:** "Between them they held one working grip."
**Problem:** Strong line — keep.
**Priority:** N/A

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 6 is largely doing the work the book asks of it: physical, sensory, controlled, with the polysyndeton-heavy violence the book has earned. The weaknesses are local, not structural. They cluster in three places: (1) explanatory tags that restate what the image already says ("not through thought but through something older and more reliable"; "in a motion that should not have been possible for…"); (2) abstract triples and negation-stacks that strain for effect ("immediate and enormous and deeply wrong"; "not shaking and not stillness and not anything he recognized"); (3) one or two run-on sentences where a stop would let a key beat land. None of these break the chapter — they just soften beats that should hit harder. The fixes are line-level, not structural.

## Top 10 Problems (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Explanatory tags after strong images** (Issues 8, 16, 17) — The narration tells the reader what just happened. Highest cumulative impact because the affected beats are central.
2. **Triple-fragment over-explaining** ("He screamed. He did not decide to scream. His body screamed because…") (Issue 14) — Softens the most physical horror beat in the chapter.
3. **Run-on burying the reveal of Hakiia's ruined hand** (Issue 22) — The reveal should land alone. Currently buried.
4. **Abstract intensifier-stacks where concrete detail follows** (Issue 13) — "immediate and enormous and deeply wrong."
5. **Wordy paragraph opener** (Issue 1) — Dilutes the opening momentum.
6. **Overwritten sound description** (Issue 18) — "a sound that came from a place where words existed."
7. **Run-on through the hatchet sequence** (Issues 12, 15) — Reader loses the beat of individual swings.
8. **Negation-pair mannerism** (Issue 28) — "not shaking and not stillness and not anything he recognized."
9. **Restated point about bleeding** (Issue 23) — "He could not stop it — too many openings, each one pouring freely."
10. **Bureaucratic/abstract phrasing in the silence-after passage** (Issue 29) — "the temporary absence of violence."

## Pattern Summary

The recurring weaknesses are all variants of one habit: after the image lands, the prose adds an interpretive clause to make sure the reader felt it. Sometimes that takes the form of a parenthetical ("a mechanical spasm that had nothing to do with life"), sometimes a negation pair ("not through thought but through something older and more reliable"), sometimes a triple fragment that explains the prior two ("His body screamed because it had been seized by something that would not release it"). The chapter trusts its imagery; it doesn't always trust the reader. A second pattern is the run-on polysyndeton extending past the point where a stop would land a beat — particularly around the wolf attack and the hand reveal. Both patterns are correctable line by line without touching the chapter's voice.

## Revision Plan (Priority Order)

1. **Cut the explanatory tags** (Issues 8, 14, 17, 16, 23). These are pure deletions; nothing rewrites itself.
2. **Break the two run-ons that bury beats** (Issues 22, 12). Add periods where the reveal needs air.
3. **Trim the abstract intensifier-stack** (Issue 13). Concrete clause after handles the work.
4. **Tighten the wordy opener** (Issue 1) and the "It was large" telling-line (Issue 4).
5. **Fix the "spread/spread" repetition** (Issue 10) and the wordy travois-tactic explainer (Issue 6).
6. **Tighten the negation pair** (Issue 28) and the overwritten sound line (Issue 18).
7. **Replace "the temporary absence of violence"** (Issue 29) with something in-register.
8. **Trim Issues 7, 19, 21, 24, 26, 27** as line edits during the same pass.
9. **Leave Issues 3, 20, 30 (Low priority) for a future polish pass.**
