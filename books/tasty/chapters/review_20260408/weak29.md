# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 29: What Hakiia Carried

## Diagnosis
Chapter 29 is restrained, concrete, and tonally disciplined. The prose earns most of its weight through sensory specifics and cumulative ritual. Weaknesses are few and localized: a handful of explanatory asides that interpret for the reader, a couple of abstracted sentences where concrete action would hit harder, and a small cluster of repetitions whose rhythm is intentional in some places and slack in others. No pervasive filler, no passive drift, no cliche spine.

## Issues

### 1. Explanatory aside undercuts the action (High)
> "and the care was already evidence that he had crossed from salvage into investigation, from accident into intent."
Problem: Narration interprets the scene for the reader in abstract register. Weakens telling-vs-showing; the care itself already carries the meaning.
Suggest: Cut the clause. Let the care stand alone.

### 2. Summary of emotion instead of behavior (Medium)
> "He was aware of his breathing in a way he had not been a moment ago."
Problem: Meta-awareness phrasing ("in a way he had not been") explains rather than renders.
Suggest: "He heard his own breathing." Or cut — the prior sentence already does the work.

### 3. Abstract metaphor where concrete would be stronger (Medium)
> "the sensation moved through him the way sound moves through a wall — felt before it is heard."
Problem: The simile explains the feeling and then glosses the simile. One beat too many.
Suggest: Trim to "the sensation moved through him the way sound moves through a wall."

### 4. Redundant gloss (Medium)
> "Documentation, not notation."
Problem: The preceding sentence already makes this distinction ("smaller, more careful, more deliberate"). The tag restates.
Suggest: Cut.

### 5. Throat-clearing / redundancy (Medium)
> "stood still for a moment, not reading the containers or setting them down, just standing in the cold water with one of them in his hand"
Problem: "stood still" + "just standing" repeats. Negative construction ("not reading...or setting them down") throat-clears.
Suggest: "He stood in the cold water with one container in his hand while the sensation moved through him..."

### 6. Abstract interpretive line (Medium)
> "because the not-reading was no longer a shelter and the reading was the only direction left."
Problem: Author-voice interpretation. Tells the reader what the moment means.
Suggest: Cut, or compress to "because not-reading was no longer shelter."

### 7. Weak tag (Low)
> "His brother's hands preserved in fiber."
Problem: Sentence fragment reaching for lyricism; "preserved in fiber" is slightly ornamental.
Suggest: "His brother's hands, still in the rope." (Low — leave if voice prefers.)

### 8. Minor redundancy (Low)
> "The rust was surface. The tools were still essentially whole."
Problem: "essentially" is a soft qualifier.
Suggest: "The tools were whole."

### 9. Slight over-explanation of diagrams (Low)
> "The diagrams were studying the mechanism by which one body's properties could enter another through consumption."
Problem: Narration summarizes what the reader has already been shown by the anatomical description.
Suggest: Trim to "The diagrams studied how one body's properties entered another."

### 10. Redundant framing line (Low)
> "The same hand. The same attention. The same refusal to do anything carelessly."
Problem: Echoes an earlier identical formulation about the knots/rope. Intentional refrain possible, but the second pass is less earned.
Suggest: Leave as refrain (intentional).

## Pattern Summary
The recurring weakness is interpretive narration — brief moments where the prose steps out of the scene to tell the reader what a gesture means. A secondary pattern is double-beat phrasing (state + restate) that drags rhythm. Both are minor within an otherwise disciplined chapter.

## Revision Plan
1. Cut or compress the explanatory asides (Issues 1, 6, 4).
2. Tighten the double-beat phrasings (Issues 5, 3, 2).
3. Trim soft qualifiers and ornamental tags (Issues 8, 9).
4. Leave intentional refrains and voice-congruent fragments (Issues 7, 10).
