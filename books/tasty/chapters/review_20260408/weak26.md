# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 26: The Market of Parts

## Per-Issue Findings

### 1. Redundant parenthetical gloss (High)
Quote: "one more face was not a face but a number — the particular anonymity Hurkuzak needed."
Problem: The dash clause explains the metaphor the reader just grasped.
Fix: Cut "— the particular anonymity Hurkuzak needed." Trust the image.

### 2. Overlong instructional sentence (High)
Quote: "He moved through it the way the voice had taught him to move through populated spaces: peripheral, unhurried, eyes forward but attention wide, reading the crowd's currents for the gaps that would let him pass without generating friction."
Problem: Sprawling, clause-stacked, and "generating friction" tips into modern register. Over-explains a simple action.
Fix: Tighten. "He moved the way the voice had taught him: peripheral, unhurried, eyes forward, attention wide, reading the crowd for gaps."

### 3. Filler qualifier (Medium)
Quote: "The voice offered its usual commentary"
Problem: "its usual" weakens; "commentary" is flat.
Fix: "The voice offered commentary" or cut the frame and let the observations run.

### 4. Throat-clearing / telling (Medium)
Quote: "He noticed the merchant's stall because the smell was wrong."
Problem: Functional but low-voltage. Then the next line restates it.
Fix: Let the smell hit first, then the stall. "The smell reached him before the stall did. Not bad — wrong."

### 5. Redundant restatement (High)
Quote: "Not bad — wrong. Salted, dried, rendered flesh, and the smell made his stomach clench because he knew it."
Problem: "because he knew it" tells what the next three sentences show.
Fix: Cut "because he knew it."

### 6. Over-explaining narration (High)
Quote: "watched Hurkuzak look and said nothing, because the customer either knew what they were looking at or they did not, and the knowing was its own qualification."
Problem: Interpretive tail flattens the moment.
Fix: Cut "because the customer... qualification." End at "said nothing."

### 7. Wordy transition (Medium)
Quote: "But the stall stayed with him — the smell, the parcels, the notation — and as he moved through the market the stall multiplied."
Problem: "stayed with him" then "multiplied" is two metaphors fighting.
Fix: "The stall multiplied as he walked."

### 8. Abstract filler (Medium)
Quote: "materials he could not see clearly but could smell through the glass"
Problem: "could not see clearly" is hedged. "smell through the glass" is implausible and dilutes.
Fix: "materials he did not need to see to name."

### 9. Repetition of "the trade" (Medium)
Quote: "The terms were the terms of a trade, and the trade was the trade of bodies like his"
Problem: Triple "trade" reads as tic rather than cadence here.
Fix: "The terms belonged to a trade in bodies like his."

### 10. Pronoun-slip / weak subject (Medium)
Quote: "A butcher's stall sat at the market's edge... He cleaned his blade between cuts"
Problem: "He" has no antecedent — the butcher was not introduced as a person.
Fix: "The butcher cleaned his blade between cuts."

### 11. Over-explained simile (Medium)
Quote: "spoken by people who had handled these goods long enough to stop seeing them."
Problem: Tacked-on moral gloss; the scene already carries it.
Fix: Cut the clause. End at "the expected margin."

### 12. Wordy catalog (Low)
Quote: "The complaint sounded the same whether the goods were wool or wheat or the liver of someone who had walked and spoken and carried a name a week ago."
Problem: Strong but long; "walked and spoken and carried a name" risks sentimentality.
Fix: Keep — earns its length. Leave.

### 13. Telling emotion (High)
Quote: "He stood at a butcher's scale and felt the weight of that without being able to name what he felt, because the feeling belonged to more than one person and the two of them did not agree on what it was."
Problem: Explains the feeling twice and names the mechanism. The chapter's whole structure already conveys this.
Fix: "He stood at the butcher's scale. The feeling belonged to more than one person."

### 14. Repetition beat (Medium)
Quote: "He did not let the thought finish. He put it away in the same place he put things he was not ready to carry, and he walked on."
Problem: Paragraph 19 already said "he did not let it finish rising." Restated close enough to feel like a tic.
Fix: Trim to "He put it away with the other things he was not ready to carry, and walked on."

### 15. Weak closing redundancy (Medium)
Quote: "The thought started again and he turned from it again. Not yet."
Problem: Third iteration of the same gesture. Diminishing returns.
Fix: Cut the line; end on the preceding paragraph. Or keep only "Not yet."

---

## Overall Diagnosis

The chapter's structure and imagery are strong; the prose weakens itself through explanatory tails — clauses that gloss what the scene already achieved. The narrator keeps stepping in after a good image to tell the reader what it meant. The withholding motif ("he did not let the thought finish") is repeated three times, once too many. A few sentences also stack clauses past their breaking point.

## Top 10 Ranked Problems

1. Explanatory tails after strong images (#1, #6, #11, #13)
2. Restated withholding beat (#14, #15)
3. Overlong instructional sentence (#2)
4. Telling-not-showing in the scale moment (#13)
5. Triple "trade" repetition (#9)
6. Missing antecedent for the butcher (#10)
7. Redundant "because he knew it" (#5)
8. Weak transitional metaphor collision (#7)
9. Hedged sensory clause (#8)
10. Filler qualifier on the voice (#3)

## Pattern Summary

The writer's recurring habit is adding an interpretive coda to sentences that had already landed. A secondary habit is repeating a gesture once more than it earns. Both flatten moments the scene had set up well.

## Revision Plan (priority order)

1. Cut interpretive tails (#1, #5, #6, #11, #13).
2. Collapse the third iteration of the withholding motif (#14, #15).
3. Tighten the instructional sentence (#2).
4. Fix the butcher antecedent (#10).
5. Resolve the "trade/trade/trade" cluster (#9).
6. Minor tightenings (#3, #4, #7, #8).
