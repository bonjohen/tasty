# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 12: What the Cabin Holds

Reviewed against `.claude/rules/weak-writing.md`. Intentional voice features (weighted sentences, restrained horror, unmarked Bleed, dark literary register) are not flagged. Only genuine weakness — slack verbs, vague abstractions, redundancy, telling-over-showing, monotone rhythm — is treated as a defect.

---

## Per-Issue Entries

### Issue 1
**Quote:** "His body told him something: he was dehydrated, weak, and stiff in every joint, and the wolf bite in his forearm throbbed with a deep, infectious heat."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing opener ("His body told him something:") plus telling-not-showing (the colon-list of conditions reads like an inventory).
**Why it weakens:** The frame "His body told him something" announces what the sentence will then dump in summary. The list flattens what should be a felt physical state.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the frame. Lead with the symptoms as concrete observations: "He was dehydrated. Weak. Every joint stiff. The wolf bite in his forearm throbbed with a deep, infectious heat."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 2
**Quote:** "He got up. The getting up took a long time and involved the same stages as before — hands, knees, a push, a second push, vertical."
**Problem:** Wordy nominalization ("The getting up took a long time and involved the same stages").
**Why it weakens:** "Getting up" as a noun phrase, plus "involved the same stages as before," is bureaucratic. The dash list afterward is the actual content.
**Suggested revision:** Strip the framing: "He got up the same way as before — hands, knees, a push, a second push, vertical."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 3
**Quote:** "He found dry wood stacked against the wall outside the back door — not much, a pile that previous visitors had left and that the overhang of the roof had kept relatively dry."
**Problem:** Run-on with weak qualifier ("relatively dry") and over-explanation of how the wood came to be there.
**Why it weakens:** The reader does not need the historical theory of who left it and why it stayed dry. The qualifier "relatively" weakens the image.
**Suggested revision:** "He found dry wood stacked outside the back door — not much, a pile left by earlier travelers and kept dry by the overhang."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 4
**Quote:** "He picked through it and separated what was edible from what was not."
**Problem:** Wordy and abstract. "What was edible from what was not" is a bureaucratic phrasing for picking through grain.
**Why it weakens:** The line floats above the action. Concrete is stronger than abstract here.
**Suggested revision:** "He picked through it, setting the moldy aside."
**Priority:** Low

### Issue 5
**Quote:** "The fat was old and slightly rancid but it would keep him alive, and keeping alive was what his body was demanding."
**Problem:** Empty intensifier ("slightly") and the second clause restates the obvious.
**Why it weakens:** "Keeping alive was what his body was demanding" tells the reader something already implied by every preceding paragraph.
**Suggested revision:** "The fat was old, rancid at the edge, but it would keep him alive."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 6
**Quote:** "His ribs screamed, his forearm pulsed, and the cut in his palm — healed badly, reopened — left prints of his own blood on his brother's chest."
**Problem:** "His ribs screamed" is a tired body-as-actor cliche.
**Why it weakens:** Generic in a chapter that otherwise renders pain with specificity.
**Suggested revision:** "His ribs caught at every breath, his forearm pulsed, and the cut in his palm — healed badly, reopened — left prints of his blood on his brother's chest."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 7
**Quote:** "He set the body down on the stone floor of the alcove and straightened the limbs as well as the stiffness allowed and pulled the furs over the face and stood back."
**Problem:** Polysyndeton run-on whose final beat ("and stood back") is muffled by the chain.
**Why it weakens:** Three "ands" in a row flatten what should be three discrete acts ending with the painful pause.
**Suggested revision:** Break for emphasis: "He set the body on the stone floor of the alcove, straightened the limbs as well as the stiffness allowed, and pulled the furs over the face. He stood back."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 8
**Quote:** "Both of these things were true and Hurkuzak could feel both of them at all times."
**Problem:** Abstract over-explanation. The two preceding sentences already carry the contradiction.
**Why it weakens:** The line interprets a moment the prose has already shown. "Could feel both of them at all times" is summary, not sensation.
**Suggested revision:** "Both were true. He felt both."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 9
**Quote:** "He could feel it enter him the way dry wood catches — not warmth yet, only the promise of it."
**Problem:** "Could feel" is a hedged sensory verb; the simile is fine but the framing weakens it.
**Why it weakens:** "Could feel" puts a layer of cognition between reader and sensation.
**Suggested revision:** "It entered him the way dry wood catches — not warmth yet, only the promise of it."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 10
**Quote:** "He slept and woke and slept and woke — the same dark room, the same cold, the same silence, and the same knowledge of what was behind the alcove door."
**Problem:** This works rhythmically — keep it. (Not flagged.)
**Priority:** —

### Issue 11
**Quote:** "Then he scratched marks on the wall above the platform with the knife, not because he expected to survive long enough for the count to matter, but because counting was something to do, and doing kept the silence from becoming pressure."
**Problem:** Slightly over-explained. The "not because…but because" construction telegraphs its own meaning.
**Why it weakens:** The narrator interprets the gesture as it happens. The image is stronger if the reason is briefer.
**Suggested revision:** "Then he scratched marks on the wall above the platform with the knife. Not because the count would matter. Because doing kept the silence from becoming pressure."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 12
**Quote:** "He cleaned it with snowmelt and wrapped it and the cleaning helped and the wrapping helped and neither solved the problem because the problem was inside the flesh and he had no tools for inside."
**Problem:** Run-on with too many "and" links. The repetition ("the cleaning helped and the wrapping helped") is a deliberate cadence but the sentence as written buckles under its own length.
**Why it weakens:** A sentence this long needs at least one structural pause to land. The final clause loses force.
**Suggested revision:** "He cleaned it with snowmelt and wrapped it. The cleaning helped. The wrapping helped. Neither reached the problem, which was inside the flesh, and for inside he had no tools."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 13
**Quote:** "His knee worked, badly, with a grinding that said something inside had settled into a new configuration — functional but wrong."
**Problem:** "Configuration" is a modern technical word that also reads as abstract; it weakens an otherwise concrete sentence.
**Why it weakens:** Both register and concreteness suffer.
**Suggested revision:** "His knee worked, badly, with a grinding that said something inside had settled wrong — usable, but wrong."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 14
**Quote:** "Everything else was worsening or not yet worsened, and the difference was food, and the food was days, and the days were running out."
**Problem:** Strong rhythm — keep it. (Not flagged.)
**Priority:** —

### Issue 15
**Quote:** "Then louder, to the room itself, as though the cabin were an audience that deserved narration."
**Problem:** "An audience that deserved narration" is the narrator interpreting instead of letting the image carry it.
**Why it weakens:** Slightly overwritten. The simile strains.
**Suggested revision:** "Then louder, to the room itself, as though the cabin were listening."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 16
**Quote:** "He was more articulate without an audience. He could build whole arguments, carry them to their conclusions, hear himself say the things he had never said to the face that had always been harder than his own."
**Problem:** "He was more articulate without an audience" tells what the next sentence shows.
**Why it weakens:** Redundant summary line.
**Suggested revision:** Cut the first sentence. Begin: "He could build whole arguments, carry them to their conclusions, hear himself say the things he had never said to the face that had always been harder than his own."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 17
**Quote:** "He paused. The fire cracked. Wind pressed against the shutters. The shutters held."
**Problem:** Strong staccato — keep it. (Not flagged.)
**Priority:** —

### Issue 18
**Quote:** "Snow fell. It fell for two days without stopping and when it stopped the world outside the cabin had risen by another foot and the door opened against a wall of white that came to his chest."
**Problem:** Mid-sentence run-on. Three independent ideas linked without a pause.
**Why it weakens:** The cadence is monotone; each clause loses its weight.
**Suggested revision:** "Snow fell. It fell for two days without stopping. When it stopped, the world outside the cabin had risen another foot, and the door opened against a wall of white that came to his chest."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 19
**Quote:** "He had been careful — smaller portions each day, stretching what was there until what was there was nothing — and the last meal was so small it was more ritual than sustenance, a few spoonfuls of thin paste."
**Problem:** "Stretching what was there until what was there was nothing" is clever but slightly precious; "more ritual than sustenance" is the kind of abstract framing the rule warns against.
**Why it weakens:** The narrator interprets where image alone would suffice.
**Suggested revision:** "He had been careful — smaller portions each day, stretching the grain until none was left — and the last meal was a few spoonfuls of thin paste."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 20
**Quote:** "His body was eating itself — he could feel it in the way his arms had gone thin, in the shaking, in the new hollows where there had been none."
**Problem:** "He could feel it in the way…" is a hedged sensory frame. The list afterward is concrete enough to stand without it.
**Why it weakens:** "Could feel" softens what should be direct.
**Suggested revision:** "His body was eating itself: arms gone thin, hands shaking, new hollows where there had been none."
**Priority:** Medium

### Issue 21
**Quote:** "He lay on the platform in the dark and listened to the fire and felt the hunger. This was the hunger that had moved in. It would remain until something fed it or the body that housed it stopped being a body."
**Problem:** "This was the hunger that had moved in" is throat-clearing; "the body that housed it stopped being a body" is overwrought paraphrasing of "until he died."
**Why it weakens:** The line interprets the hunger instead of letting the next paragraph render it.
**Suggested revision:** "He lay on the platform in the dark and listened to the fire and felt the hunger. It had moved in. It would stay until something fed it or he stopped being a body to house it."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 22
**Quote:** "He lay in the dark and the dark was complete because the fire had burned low and the candles were too precious to waste and the only light in the room was the faint glow of embers."
**Problem:** Run-on of four "and" clauses. The cause-and-effect chain ("complete because…and…and…") is over-explained.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence buckles. The image of embers as the only light is buried.
**Suggested revision:** "He lay in the dark. The fire had burned low. The candles were too precious to waste. The only light in the room was the faint glow of embers."
**Priority:** High

### Issue 23
**Quote:** "He did not think the thought."
**Problem:** Strong — keep it. (Not flagged.)
**Priority:** —

## Overall Diagnosis

The chapter is largely strong work in the book's intended register: weighted, repetitive in a controlled way, sensory, and earned. The recurring weakness is over-explanation — sentences that interpret a moment the surrounding prose has already shown, or that frame a sensation with hedged sensory verbs ("could feel," "his body told him") instead of rendering it directly. A second pattern is run-on polysyndeton: chains of "and" clauses that begin as deliberate cadence but exceed the load-bearing capacity of a single sentence and flatten the rhythm. Both problems are surgical, not structural; the chapter does not need rewriting, only the removal of the small interpretive scaffolding that the strong sentences are already capable of supporting on their own.

---

## Top 10 Problems Ranked by Impact

1. **Run-on sentence chains held together by "and"** — Issues 12, 18, 22. The most consistent rhythmic flaw. Each example weakens the punch of the surrounding controlled cadence.
2. **Interpretive summary lines after a fully-rendered moment** — Issues 8, 16, 21. These tell the reader what the prose has just shown.
3. **Hedged sensory verbs ("could feel")** — Issues 9, 20. Inserts cognition between reader and sensation.
4. **Throat-clearing frames** ("His body told him something:") — Issue 1. Announces an inventory the sentence then dumps.
5. **Wordy nominalizations** ("The getting up took a long time and involved…") — Issue 2.
6. **Polysyndeton flattening final beats** — Issue 7. Three "ands" muffle the painful pause.
7. **Modern abstract diction** ("configuration," "audience that deserved narration") — Issues 13, 15. Out-of-register slips.
8. **Empty qualifiers** ("slightly," "relatively") — Issues 3, 5.
9. **Body-as-actor cliche** ("his ribs screamed") — Issue 6.
10. **Over-narrated motivation** ("not because…but because…and doing kept…") — Issue 11.

---

## Pattern Summary

Three recurring weaknesses dominate:

1. **Interpretive scaffolding.** The narrator periodically steps in to explain a moment the scene has already rendered. The strong sentences in this chapter do not need help; the helping lines weaken them by saying the obvious thing twice.
2. **Polysyndeton fatigue.** The "and…and…and" cadence is a real and earned voice feature in this book — but it has a load limit. Past three or four links, the rhythm collapses into monotone and the final beat loses its weight.
3. **Hedged sensation.** "He could feel," "his body told him," "this was the hunger that had moved in" — small frames that put a layer of cognition between the reader and the body. The chapter is otherwise direct enough not to need them.

---

## Revision Plan in Priority Order

1. **High priority — collapse the over-explained interpretive lines.** Issues 8, 16, 21, 22, 12. Each is a one- or two-sentence surgical cut or re-break that lets the surrounding prose carry the meaning.
2. **Medium priority — strip throat-clearing frames and hedged sensory verbs.** Issues 1, 2, 9, 20. Remove "his body told him," "the getting up," "he could feel," and let the concrete observations stand.
3. **Medium priority — break the run-on polysyndeton chains.** Issues 7, 18. Replace one or two "ands" with periods to restore the load-bearing rhythm.
4. **Medium priority — small concrete fixes.** Issues 3, 5, 6, 11, 13, 15, 19. Cut empty qualifiers, replace "configuration," replace "ribs screamed," tighten the over-narrated gestures.
5. **Low priority — minor wordiness.** Issue 4. A single-line tightening.

Total: roughly fifteen surgical edits. No structural changes, no scene reordering, no new prose.
