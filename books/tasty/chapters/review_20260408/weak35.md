# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 35: What the Net Holds

## Diagnosis
The chapter is structurally strong, POV-controlled, and earns its dread. Remaining weaknesses cluster around explain-after-image: nearly every concrete action is followed by an interpretive sentence that names what the image already showed. A handful of doubled beats and one self-contradicting simile also drag. Voice is intact; cuts are surgical.

---

## Issues

### 1. Throat-clearing aside about compounds — Medium
**Quote:** "A trading compound of the kind that sat along every major road in the eastern territories: a walled enclosure where merchants and travelers stopped to water animals, trade news, and sleep behind something stronger than a tent."
**Problem:** Encyclopedic gloss stalls the opening tension.
**Revision:** Cut the colon-clause; let "trading compound" carry the meaning.

### 2. Overlong smell explanation — Medium
**Quote:** "the smell that rose from it was sharp and sweet and wrong, the kind of wrong that lives in the back of the throat: a sedation draught, brewed strong enough to bring down something harder to kill than a man."
**Problem:** "the kind of wrong that lives in the back of the throat" is soft, and the colon-clause names what the image already implies.
**Revision:** Tighten to one concrete clause.

### 3. Telling the obvious — Medium
**Quote:** "They were not trying to kill him."
**Problem:** The vials, the cord, the patience all show this. Stating it flattens the realization.
**Revision:** Cut.

### 4. Self-contradicting simile — Medium
**Quote:** "He moved through the second man the way water moves through a gap — not around but through"
**Problem:** Water through a gap goes around, not through; the figure undoes itself.
**Revision:** Drop the simile and render the action.

### 5. Run-on with explanatory closer — Low
**Quote:** "The second vial broke in the man's pocket and the sedation soaked through his clothes and the man's legs buckled as his own weapon did to him what it had been meant to do to Hurkuzak."
**Problem:** The closing clause names the irony the image already carried.
**Revision:** Break and trim.

### 6. Repeated "He hit" beats — Medium
**Quote:** "He hit the third man. The hit was slower than it should have been. He hit him again."
**Problem:** Three "hit" beats in a row read as choreography rather than urgency.
**Revision:** Vary verbs; compress.

### 7. Doubled "at once" — Medium
**Quote:** "His legs did not buckle at once. His vision did not go dark at once."
**Problem:** Adjacent sentences with the same end-phrase flatten rhythm.
**Revision:** Vary the second beat.

### 8. Thematic bumper line — High
**Quote:** "He was meat with a consciousness, and the chain was pulling him in."
**Problem:** Direct theme statement. The chapter has already shown this. Stating it explains the dread away.
**Revision:** Cut, or compress to image only.

### 9. Doubled fade — Medium
**Quote:** "the strength in his arms began to leave him, muscle by muscle, until his grip was strong, then weak, then nothing."
**Problem:** "muscle by muscle" plus the strong/weak/nothing tricolon double-counts the same image.
**Revision:** Pick one.

### 10. Procedural footnote at peak tension — Low
**Quote:** "the work of people who had done this before, who knew the order of it: down first, bound while the draught held, then doubled before the body began to burn it away."
**Problem:** Procedural aside pulls out of POV at the worst moment.
**Revision:** Trim the colon-list.

### 11. Four-sentence vision-narrowing — Medium
**Quote:** "His vision was narrowing. The courtyard was becoming smaller, the walls closing in, the sky above contracting to a point of light that was receding."
**Problem:** Four sentences for one effect; close to "darkness claimed him" territory the project disavows.
**Revision:** One sharp image.

### 12. Polysyndeton inventory — Low
**Quote:** "strikes that were efficient and frightening and that left bruises and cuts on the man's arms and torso without finishing him."
**Problem:** List drains force.
**Revision:** Compress.

### 13. "Weeks ago." undercut — Low
**Quote:** "The options he thought he carried had been closed days ago. Weeks ago."
**Problem:** "Weeks ago" undercuts rather than escalates.
**Revision:** Cut "Weeks ago."

## Pattern Summary
- Image then gloss: action followed by an interpretive sentence.
- Doubled beats: parallel sentences that say the same thing twice.
- Procedural or encyclopedic asides at high-tension moments.
- Occasional simile reach where plain rendering would land harder.

## Revision Plan
1. Cut the thematic bumper (#8).
2. Cut the obvious tells (#3, #13).
3. Compress the darkness into one image (#11).
4. Trim the compound and smell asides (#1, #2).
5. Replace the contradictory water simile (#4).
6. De-stutter "hit" sequence and "at once" pair (#6, #7).
7. Pick one of the doubled fade images (#9).
