# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 31: The Knife Behind the Door

## Per-Issue Entries

### Issue 1 — Abstract, clinical phrasing (Medium)
**Quote:** "The remoteness was isolation. The winter sealed the environment — no escape, no rescue."
**Problem:** "Environment" is a modern, clinical abstraction that drops out of the chapter's tactile register.
**Revision:** Replace "environment" with a concrete noun native to the world ("the country," "the valley," "the place").

### Issue 2 — Weak verb / vague motion (Medium)
**Quote:** "His body needed to move. He walked three steps from the materials and stopped and walked back and stood over them..."
**Problem:** "His body needed to move" is a telling beat that explains what the following action already shows.
**Revision:** Cut the throat-clearing sentence; let the action carry it.

### Issue 3 — Abstract noun stacking (Medium)
**Quote:** "He could not name what drove it — attention or apology or hunger."
**Problem:** "Drove it" is vague; the abstraction list is fine but the verb is slack.
**Revision:** Tighten to "He could not name it — attention, apology, hunger."

### Issue 4 — Redundancy (Low)
**Quote:** "He put the fragments down the way a man handles something that has cut him and might cut him again. He placed each one on the ground and weighted each with a stone and the weighting was precise..."
**Problem:** "Put down" and "placed each one on the ground" restate the same action.
**Revision:** Collapse to a single placement, keep the precision beat.

### Issue 5 — Filler qualifier (Low)
**Quote:** "The thing producing the pressure was not fully a person and not fully not a person."
**Problem:** "Fully" twice reads as hedging in a chapter otherwise committed to plain declaration.
**Revision:** "The thing producing the pressure was not a person and not not a person." — cleaner paradox.

### Issue 6 — Weak verb "arrived" (Low)
**Quote:** "The impulse to destroy them arrived with the force of a physical need"
**Problem:** "Arrived with the force of" is a common literary-prose cliche and slightly inflated.
**Revision:** "The impulse to destroy them came as physical need" — shorter, harder.

### Issue 7 — Telling emotion (Medium)
**Quote:** "He could not determine which."
**Problem:** After a long, weighted paragraph, "determine" is the wrong register — bureaucratic, flattens the grief.
**Revision:** "He could not tell which." Plainer, closer to the body.

### Issue 8 — Abstract "materials" bloat (Low)
**Quote:** "He walked three steps from the materials and stopped and walked back and stood over them and looked down at the pages and the containers and the fragments of a design that had been meant to kill him."
**Problem:** Sentence runs long and loops; "materials" then "pages and the containers and the fragments" restates inventory.
**Revision:** Trim the loop, keep one catalog.

## Diagnosis

The chapter is disciplined and largely earns its length. Its repetitions are deliberate (the brother/weaker litany, the truth/truth stacking) and should not be flattened. The real weaknesses are a handful of abstract or clinical word choices ("environment," "determine," "fully") and one or two throat-clearing action beats that explain what the prose then shows. Surgical trims only.

## Pattern Summary
Occasional drift into abstract/clinical nouns and verbs ("environment," "determine," "drove it") where the chapter's default register is concrete and bodily. A few throat-clearing sentences that pre-explain action.

## Revision Plan
1. Swap "environment" for a concrete world-noun.
2. Cut "His body needed to move."
3. Tighten "could not name what drove it" and "not fully... not fully not."
4. Replace "determine" with "tell."
5. Trim the walked-back loop and the doubled placement.
6. Shorten "arrived with the force of a physical need."
