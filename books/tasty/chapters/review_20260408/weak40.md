# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 40: What the Hunter Sees

## Per-Issue Findings

### 1. Redundant doubling of "two days" — Medium
Quote: "Vark had been inside it for two days and the two days had taught her more..."
Problem: Echoic repetition that adds no rhythm, just bulk.
Fix: "Vark had been inside it two days, and those two days had taught her..." or collapse to one clause.

### 2. Throat-clearing / overexplained "clean" riff — Medium
Quote: "Not clean the way a butcher's floor was clean — scrubbed, adequate for the next carcass. Clean the way a physician's instruments were clean."
Problem: The contrast lands, but the third "clean" plus the gloss on butcher floors slows the paragraph. One comparison would do the work.
Fix: Trim "scrubbed, adequate for the next carcass." Let the physician comparison carry it.

### 3. Vague "something else, something that held the air cold past reason" — Low
Problem: Doubled "something" is a tic; could be tightened.
Fix: "packed earth and straw and something that held the air cold past reason."

### 4. Filler "She had expected competence. She had not expected elegance." — Low
Keep — earns its place as a beat. No change.

### 5. Clinical drift: "The delivery's quality had been verified by people whose instruments and methods..." — Medium
Problem: Long, abstract sentence after a concrete passage. "Living subject" lands but the run-up is bureaucratic mush.
Fix: Tighten: "Tessivane's people had verified the delivery with instruments she had never seen used on a living body. Their finding confirmed what she already knew: the subject was extraordinary."

### 6. "She had been paid" paragraph — Low
Functional. Keep.

### 7. "drove the effect deeper and cleaner than she had known it to go" + "She could feel the difference. The meal entered her system with a clarity..." — Medium
Problem: Three sentences saying the same thing — the meal hit harder than usual. Repetition without escalation.
Fix: Cut "She could feel the difference." Let the next sentence carry it.

### 8. "reflexes that were not his, that had come into him from the dead brother the way a fire takes when the conditions are right" — Low
Keep — strong image, earns its length.

### 9. "The capabilities were real and she had priced them correctly. The delivery had demonstrated that." — Medium
Problem: Second sentence restates the first.
Fix: Delete "The delivery had demonstrated that."

### 10. "The morning was quiet. The principal would conduct his examination in the days ahead. She was available, as the contract specified. Present. Ready." — Medium
Problem: "Present. Ready." after "She was available" is throat-clearing. The fragments don't add pressure; they pad.
Fix: Cut "Present. Ready."

### 11. "It was small. A guard who should have been..." paragraph — Low
Works. Keep.

### 12. "She noted it. She did not act on it." followed by explanation — Low
Functional. Keep.

### 13. "Everything was ordinary." — Low
Earns its place as a button. Keep.

### 14. Second irregularity sentence — Medium
Quote: "A sound from the interior — not an alarm, not a shout, but a percussive impact that carried through the stone the way a heavy object carries through a floor. A thud. One. Then nothing. The kind of sound a body makes when it hits stone from standing height without attempting to arrest its fall."
Problem: "A thud. One." after the long simile is good rhythm; the final sentence then explains the thud, deflating it.
Fix: Cut the final explanatory sentence, OR cut "A thud. One. Then nothing." and keep the explanatory one. Recommend cutting the explanation — the image is sharper.

### 15. "The guard was not at the junction because the guard was on the floor of the junction." — Low
Strong line. Keep.

### 16. "He was alive. Breathing." then "The breathing was shallow and irregular but present." — Low
Slight redundancy with "Breathing" and "breathing... present" but the rhythm works. Keep.

### 17. "She moved down the corridor toward the open door with the crossbow aligned and her breathing regulated." — Low
Keep.

### 18. "Dead guard. Neck broken. Weapon partially drawn — response time perhaps one second from alert to incapacitation. No weapon marks. No blood. His hand was still on the grip." — Low
Strong tactical compression. Keep.

### 19. "The subject in the study. His capabilities — the speed, the strength, the dead brother's combat instincts operating in him like a second set of hands." — Low
Keep.

### 20. The long restraint-chair sentence — High
Quote: "...the restraints had been opened — not broken, not torn, but unbuckled, the treated lining of the restraints peeled back and laid across the chair's arm with the deliberateness of a person who had studied the mechanism and understood it and removed it without urgency because the urgency was not in the removal but in what came after."
Problem: Run-on. The closing clause ("because the urgency was not in the removal but in what came after") explains the image and weakens it. The chiastic phrasing is overwritten.
Fix: End at "without urgency." Cut the explanatory tail.

### 21. "moving through it with the integrated purpose of a being who had read the facility the way a hunter reads terrain — the way she read terrain — and had converted that reading into action" — Medium
Problem: "converted that reading into action" is abstract and bureaucratic after the strong "the way she read terrain" beat.
Fix: End at "the way she read terrain."

### 22. "The stronghold had gone quiet — not silence, but the absence of routine. No footsteps... No sounds... No voices. The quiet of a machine that has stopped." — Low
Keep — the catalog earns the closing image.

### 23. "felt something she had not felt in a very long time" + body-evidence sentences — Medium
Problem: The opening sentence tells the emotion, then the next sentences show it. The telling sentence is unnecessary and contradicts "show, don't summarize."
Fix: Cut "and felt something she had not felt in a very long time." Let the body do it.

### 24. "She had always known where the prey was. What it would do. That knowing had been absolute. She did not know where he was now." — Low
Works. Keep.

### 25. "She entered the deeper corridor." right after "She moved toward the deeper chambers." — Medium
Problem: Two sentences saying she went deeper.
Fix: Cut "She entered the deeper corridor." Let the torchlight sentence carry the move.

## Overall Diagnosis
Strong chapter. Tight POV, controlled tactical voice, restrained horror. Weaknesses are local: occasional doubling, a few sentences that explain images the prose has already delivered, and one run-on near the climax. No structural problem.

## Pattern Summary
Recurring tic: a strong concrete sentence followed by an abstract sentence that explains it. The chapter is at its best when it cuts on the image. Secondary tic: mild doubling (two days / two days, deeper / deeper, capabilities real / delivery demonstrated).

2. Delete restated sentences in #7, #9, #10, #25.

4. Leave all keep-marked items alone — they are voice, not weakness.
