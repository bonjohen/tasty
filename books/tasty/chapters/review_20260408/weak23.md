# Weak-Writing Review — Chapter 23: The Familiar Tongue

## Per-Issue Entries

### 1. Redundant establishment tag — "That was established now."
**Quote:** "His body could not be trusted in public. That was established now."
**Problem:** Throat-clearing / unnecessary explanation. The prior sentence already establishes it.
**Revision:** Cut "That was established now."
**Priority:** Medium

### 2. Repetition of the "established" tag
**Quote:** "Hurkuzak had followed the way he had always followed. He had not decided to follow. That was established now too."
**Problem:** Repetition of a tic ("established now") across the chapter. Flattens rhythm and calls attention to itself.
**Revision:** Cut "That was established now too." The two prior sentences carry it.
**Priority:** High

### 3. Overwriting / saying it twice — market behind him
**Quote:** "The market was behind him but the market was not behind him."
**Problem:** The next three sentences already render this. The abstract frame before the concrete images is throat-clearing.
**Revision:** Drop the abstract frame; open directly with the faces, the sound, the hands.
**Priority:** Medium

### 4. Wordy qualifier
**Quote:** "temporary, because solitude was a condition he was no longer sure he occupied."
**Problem:** "a condition he was no longer sure he occupied" is abstract and wordy where a cleaner line would cut.
**Revision:** "temporary, because he was no longer sure he was alone."
**Priority:** Medium

### 5. Redundant phrasing
**Quote:** "Inside him, regardless of what was outside, was the company of one"
**Problem:** "regardless of what was outside" is filler; the contrast is already made by cabin/road.
**Revision:** "Inside him was the company of one"
**Priority:** Medium

### 6. Overexplained reaction
**Quote:** "The criticism arrived and the old architecture of obedience activated — adjust before the next correction lands."
**Problem:** "the old architecture of obedience activated" explains what the behavioral beat already shows. Telling on top of showing.
**Revision:** Let the adjustment itself carry it: cut the explanatory clause.
**Priority:** Medium

### 7. Redundant clarification
**Quote:** "The phrase had arrived already shaped — not retrieved, not recalled."
**Problem:** "not retrieved, not recalled" restates "already shaped."
**Revision:** Trim to "The phrase had arrived already shaped."
**Priority:** Medium

### 8. Repetitive silence echo
**Quote:** "He stood in the forest and listened to the silence that followed the phrase and the silence was not empty."
**Problem:** Run-on via "and...and." The repetition of "silence" is intentional but the sentence structure sags.
**Revision:** Break into two: "He stood and listened to the silence that followed. It was not empty."
**Priority:** Medium

### 9. Throat-clearing opener
**Quote:** "The terrain required attention, and the attention was a relief."
**Problem:** Echoes an identical construction earlier ("The forest asked nothing of him, and the nothing was a relief"). Second use weakens the first.
**Revision:** Cut this sentence; the walking resumes without commentary.
**Priority:** Medium

### 10. Redundant scene tag
**Quote:** "The forest was spring forest now"
**Problem:** Already told us "He traveled through spring forest" earlier in the chapter. Restating is throat-clearing.
**Revision:** "The forest was wet underfoot, the canopy..."
**Priority:** Medium

### 11. Wordy / vague
**Quote:** "moving by a judgment his mind issued without being consulted."
**Problem:** Abstract and slightly convoluted. "without being consulted" is cute but soft.
**Revision:** "moving by a judgment his mind did not issue."
**Priority:** Low

### 12. Monotone list rhythm
**Quote:** "feet placing themselves on the slick bark with the weight distribution of someone who had crossed a thousand such logs."
**Problem:** "weight distribution" tips clinical; the line is long and its punch is blunted.
**Revision:** "feet placing themselves on the slick bark with the ease of someone who had crossed a thousand such logs."
**Priority:** Low

### 13. Explaining the emotion
**Quote:** "He told himself it was exhaustion. The mind starved of rest made things."
**Problem:** Fine, but the follow-up "The explanation was plausible. Grief invented what it needed." is a third restatement of the same idea.
**Revision:** Cut "The explanation was plausible."
**Priority:** Medium

### 14. Weak abstract close
**Quote:** "the not-empty was terrible and comforting in equal measure"
**Problem:** "in equal measure" is a stock phrase; drains the line.
**Revision:** "the not-empty was terrible and comforting at once"
**Priority:** Low

### 15. Throat-clearing
**Quote:** "He did not speak. He did not name what sat beside the fire with him. He was not ready — but the pretending was costing him more each day than the naming would cost all at once."
**Problem:** Third time in the chapter we're told he will not name the someone. The idea is already landed twice ("He did not name the someone. Not yet." and "the man whose name he would not yet say").
**Revision:** Trim the middle repetition; keep the first and last.
**Priority:** Medium

---

## Overall Diagnosis

A strong, disciplined chapter whose weaknesses are small and recurring: a handful of throat-clearing tags ("That was established now"), a tendency to restate an abstract frame right before the concrete image that renders it, and two or three repeated gestures about not-naming-the-someone that would hit harder at two beats than at three. The voice, imagery, and restraint are intact; the prose would tighten by cutting roughly a dozen short phrases.

## Top 10 Ranked

1. Repeated "That was established now" tic (Issues 1, 2)
2. "He did not name..." repeated three times (Issue 15)
3. Abstract frame before concrete image at market (Issue 3)
4. "Grief invented what it needed" trio overstates (Issue 13)
5. "spring forest now" restatement (Issue 10)
6. "architecture of obedience activated" telling (Issue 6)
7. "not retrieved, not recalled" restatement (Issue 7)
8. "and...and" sag in silence sentence (Issue 8)
9. "condition he was no longer sure he occupied" wordiness (Issue 4)
10. "regardless of what was outside" filler (Issue 5)

## Pattern Summary

- Restatement of an idea in abstract after it has already landed concretely.
- A signature tag ("established now") reused for emphasis where once would do.
- The non-naming motif hit one beat too many.
- Occasional soft wordiness in otherwise precise sentences.

## Revision Plan

1. Strip both "established now" tags.
2. Trim the middle non-naming beat.
3. Cut the abstract market frame; let the images carry it.
4. Trim "The explanation was plausible."
5. Tighten "spring forest now" and "regardless of what was outside."
6. Drop "the old architecture of obedience activated —".
7. Drop "not retrieved, not recalled."
8. Split the "silence...and...and" sentence.
9. Tighten "a condition he was no longer sure he occupied."
