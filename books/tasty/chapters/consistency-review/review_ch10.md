# Setting Consistency Review — Chapter 10: The Carrying

**Chapter:** Ch 10
**Section:** 5 — The March to the Cabin
**Setting:** Pre-industrial dark fantasy. Wagons, sleds, bladed weapons, bows, traps. No engines, firearms, or electricity.
**Review standard:** `poor-consistency.md`
**Date:** 2026-04-06

---

## Issue-by-Issue Findings

---

### Issue 1

**Passage:**
> "the mathematics of it"

**Problem:** Modern scientific/analytical register.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Mathematics" as a metaphor for cold practical logic is a modern intellectual register. A pre-industrial character with no formal scientific training would not reach for "mathematics" as the right word to describe Hakiia's reasoning about the mercy killing. The concept is available — reckoning, figuring, counting the cost — but this particular word choice carries the flavor of Enlightenment rationalism and quantitative thinking.

**Suggested direction:** "You were right about the reckoning of it" or "You were right about the accounting of it" or simply "the logic of it" (which is older in register). Alternatively: "You were right about what it meant — the cold addition of it."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 2

**Passage:**
> "the body announcing that it had a separate opinion about what happened next"

**Problem:** Modern psychological/bureaucratic idiom — "separate opinion."

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Opinion" as applied to the body's autonomous signals is a modern framing, drawing on the vocabulary of deliberation, consensus, and dissenting views. It leans into an intellectualized body-mind split that feels more contemporary than pre-industrial. The sentence's tone is intentionally wry and distanced, which suits the register, but "opinion" is the weak link.

**Suggested direction:** "the body announcing that it had its own argument about what happened next" — or "the body announcing that it had a different idea about what happened next." "Argument" or "claim" keeps the wit while pulling back from the formal deliberative register of "opinion."

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 3

**Passage:**
> "the cauterization had done its work"

**Problem:** Clinical/medical register for a pre-industrial field wound.

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Cauterization" is the correct technical term for sealing a wound by burning it, and it does exist in pre-modern medical vocabulary. However, in this narrative context — Hurkuzak looking at his dead brother's hand in the cold — the clinical precision of "cauterization" feels like a register intrusion. The narrative elsewhere is physical and direct. "Where the fire had sealed it" or "where the iron had closed it" would root the description in how such a procedure would actually be understood and recalled by someone who had done it in the field with a blade or brand, not a physician's terminology.

**Suggested direction:** "the dark sealed places where the fire had closed it" or "where the iron had done its work" or "where the burning had done its work."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 4

**Passage:**
> "the specific emptiness that follows a word spoken to someone who cannot hear"

**Problem:** Clinical/analytical narration — "specific emptiness."

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Specific" here is a modern analytical qualifier that belongs to a diagnostic or technical register. A character in this world feeling the particular quality of silence after speaking to the dead would not parse it as "specific" in this way — the word imports a categorizing, taxonomic impulse. The surrounding sentence is excellent and deeply in the world's register; "specific" is the single intruder.

**Suggested direction:** "the deep emptiness that follows" or "the hollow that follows" or simply "the silence after a word spoken to someone who cannot hear" — the phrase works without the qualifier. The qualifier weakens it.

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 5

**Passage:**
> "His mind did, and his mind was the least useful part of him."

**Problem:** Modern therapeutic/cognitive register — framing the mind as the "least useful part."

**Why it breaks immersion:** This is a light intrusion — the phrasing is clever and earned, and it works emotionally. But "least useful part" frames the self in a quasi-instrumental, efficiency-driven way that sits slightly outside the pre-industrial register. It is the kind of dry self-assessment a modern person makes. A character from this world would more likely think of himself in terms of his will, or his spirit, or what he can manage — not in terms of cognitive utility.

**Suggested direction:** "his mind was the part of him doing the least good" or "his mind was the least of what he needed." These preserve the self-deprecating irony without the modern management-of-resources undertone.

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 6

**Passage:**
> "Not that I did it. That you were right. That the cruel thing and the kind thing were the same thing..."

**Problem:** Modern moral-philosophical framing.

**Why it breaks immersion:** The passage works psychologically and emotionally, and the distinction Hurkuzak draws is real and earned. However, the abstracted formulation — "the cruel thing and the kind thing were the same thing" — has the cadence of modern ethical reflection. A pre-industrial character under this much duress and from this kind of cultural background would more likely circle around the idea obliquely, through concrete memory, rather than arriving at an explicit philosophical proposition. The line risks feeling like the author's moral analysis placed into the character's mouth, which is especially visible because the rest of the chapter rigorously avoids that move.

**Suggested direction:** The idea can survive as concrete recollection: "You were right. The blade was right. And I could not make it be anything else, no matter what I called it." This preserves the emotional truth without the abstracting move.

**Priority:** High

---

### Issue 7

**Passage:**
> "The silence absorbed the words the way the snow absorbed the blood."

**Problem:** No setting-consistency issue here — this is flagged for the opposite reason.

This simile is excellent and fully in-world: direct, physical, earned. The comparison is drawn from materials and processes available in the setting. No issue found.

**Note for record only.** Not an immersion break.

---

### Issue 8

**Passage:**
> "He looked ahead and saw, through the trees, a shape that was the cabin — the straight lines, the right angles, the suggestion of a roof under snow."

**Problem:** Architectural description ("straight lines," "right angles") slightly modern in analytical register.

**Why it breaks immersion:** This is a very mild intrusion. "Straight lines" and "right angles" are not technological concepts, but their combined use here reads as a geometric analysis a pre-industrial character might not spontaneously reach for while hallucinating from exhaustion and grief. Hurkuzak would more likely recognize the cabin's shape through its specific function markers — a chimney shape, a wall face, the darkness of a door — not through abstract geometric description.

**Suggested direction:** "a shape that was the cabin — a wall face catching the light, the dark slot of a door, the horizontal of a snow-held roof." This roots the recognition in what he would actually see and know to look for.

**Priority:** Low

---

### Issue 9

**Passage:**
> "the same indeterminate distance it had always occupied, receding as he approaches"

**Note:** Minor tense shift — "he approaches" vs. past tense throughout. Not a setting-consistency issue but worth noting as a copyediting flag.

**Priority:** Low (copy, not consistency)

---

### Issue 10

**Passage:**
> "the mechanical repetition of effort that had ground his mind down to its functional minimum"

**Problem:** Modern industrial/mechanical register — "functional minimum."

**Why it breaks immersion:** "Functional minimum" is engineering language. The phrase describes cognitive degradation in terms borrowed from machine operation — a minimum necessary level of function. While the sentence is otherwise effective, this phrase imports a mechanistic, industrial vocabulary into a character who has no frame of reference for machinery or functional thresholds in the technical sense. The broader sentence is strong; this phrase is the weak word-choice.

**Suggested direction:** "the grinding effort that had worn his mind down to its bare kernel" or "that had stripped his mind to the one thing it still needed to know" or "to what it could no longer refuse."

**Priority:** Medium

---

### Issue 11

**Passage:**
> "A fire required hands and focus and material. He had barely the first, none of the second."

**Problem:** List logic phrased in a slightly modern accounting register.

**Why it breaks immersion:** Mild. The sentence structure — presenting requirements as enumerated conditions and then auditing them — has a faintly bureaucratic/analytical clarity that sits slightly outside the register of a man barely conscious. The rhythm is too precise for the stated cognitive state. The effect is small but noticeable next to the chapter's otherwise well-maintained degraded perception.

**Suggested direction:** "A fire needed hands and a mind that could hold a thought. He had neither well enough." Or let it be shorter: "A fire needed what he no longer had."

**Priority:** Low

---

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 10 is a strong piece of writing, and its setting consistency is largely solid. This is a pre-industrial world and the chapter is set entirely outdoors in deep winter — no technology beyond the travois, the furs, and the strip of leather Hurkuzak uses for repair. There are no anachronistic objects, no modern institutions, no mistaken technology. The physical world is correctly rendered throughout.

The consistency problems are almost entirely at the register level — moments where the narration slides into modern analytical, clinical, or industrial vocabulary. The chapter's literary distance (third-person close, coolly observational) makes this especially visible, because the prose otherwise earns its plainness. When a phrase like "functional minimum" or "the mathematics of it" appears, it jars against the surrounding language in a way that signals the author's modern frame of reference rather than the world's.

The single High-priority issue (Issue 6) is structural rather than purely lexical: Hurkuzak's spoken dialogue arrives at a too-clean moral proposition that breaks the chapter's own principle of showing rather than analyzing.

## Pattern Summary

The chapter's recurring weakness is register drift in analytical or descriptive moments. When the narration steps back from pure sensory experience to analyze or categorize — the quality of a silence, the state of a mind, the nature of an injury, the geometry of a hallucinated shape — it reaches for modern vocabulary. The problem is not conceptual (the world contains grief, exhaustion, field medicine, architecture); the problem is linguistic. The author defaults to modern analytical precision when the character's frame of reference would produce something rougher, more concrete, and more embedded in material experience.

This pattern is consistent with a narrator who is very close to the character's cognition but occasionally slips into the author's educated modern voice when the content requires abstraction or categorization.

## Revision Plan (Priority Order)

1. **High — Rewrite the moral proposition in the spoken monologue.** Replace the abstract formulation ("the cruel thing and the kind thing were the same thing") with a concrete, embodied statement that arrives at the same emotional truth through action or memory rather than proposition.

2. **Medium — Replace "cauterization."** Use the physical process ("fire," "iron," "burning") instead of the clinical term. The hand is being remembered, not medically charted.

3. **Medium — Replace "functional minimum."** Find a phrase that describes cognitive stripping without industrial register.

4. **Medium — Remove "specific" from "the specific emptiness."** The qualifier weakens the phrase and imports an analytical register that does not belong there.

5. **Medium — Revise "the mathematics of it."** Use "the reckoning" or "the accounting" or "the cold logic" — anything that preserves the meaning without the quantitative-science register.

6. **Low — All remaining issues.** Address "a separate opinion," "least useful part," "straight lines, right angles," "He had barely the first, none of the second," and the tense shift in the hallucination passage, in that order, as the draft allows.

---

## Failure Mode Assessment

This chapter is not failing from deeper worldbuilding inconsistency or narrator-voice drift. The world is correctly imagined throughout. The objects, landscape, weather, social situation, and cultural context are all consistent with the pre-industrial setting. The problems are isolated wording choices — moments where analytical or clinical precision is borrowed from modern English when the register should be plainer, more material, or more approximate. These are surface fixes. The architecture beneath them is sound.
