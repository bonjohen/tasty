1. Winter hunt and brother dynamic
   Hakiia takes his younger brother Hurkuzak into the frozen wilds on a winter hunting trip, where their old pattern is clear: Hakiia is strong, harsh, and gifted; Hurkuzak is kind, weak, and bad at fighting.

2. The Cliff (was: The mine)
   Their sled when off a cliff ten kilometers from a remote cabin, destroying it, scattering supplies, injuring both brothers, and leaving Hakiia with two broken legs.

3. The wolves
   As they struggle through the snow toward shelter, wolves attack. The brothers survive, but the fight leaves Hurkuzak badly battered and Hakiia critically wounded beyond hope of recovery.

4. The mercy killing
   Knowing he faces a slow death from cold, pain, and infection, Hakiia demands a mercy killing. Hurkuzak resists, then grants it, creating the central wound of the story.

5. The march to the cabin
   Hurkuzak carries Hakiia's body through the snow to the cabin, collapses beside it, and falls unconscious from exhaustion, injury, and grief.

6. Silence, hunger, and taboo
   Snowed in and starving, Hurkuzak survives for days, then weeks, until hunger, guilt, and isolation drive him to consume Hakiia piece by piece.

7. Change in body, change in mind
   As the weeks pass, Hurkuzak becomes stronger, faster, harder to kill, and resistant to the cold. He also begins hearing a second inner voice he believes is madness.

8. Return to the world
   When spring finally lets him leave the mountain, Hurkuzak returns to civilization changed, unstable, and terrified that anyone might discover what he has done.

9. Captain Sella Vark marks him
   Demon hunter Captain Sella Vark identifies Hurkuzak as valuable prey, then grows fascinated when she sees his unnatural healing, speed, and bursts of power.

10. Public eruption
    Cornered in public, Hurkuzak unleashes a terrifying surge of borrowed violence and skill, drawing wide attention and marking himself as something far stranger than rare meat.

11. Mental reconciliation
    As the inner voice grows clearer, Hurkuzak begins to reinterpret Hakiia. He sees that his brother's cruelty was real, but so was his rough, twisted form of love. Hakiia truly believed he was trying to make Hurkuzak strong enough to survive.

12. Return to the wreck
    Quite by accident, Hurkuzak returns to the site of the destroyed vehicle and finds ritual components, notes, and hidden materials proving Hakiia knew about the forbidden sibling-consumption rite before the trip.

13. The terrible realization
    From the materials in the wreck, Hurkuzak realizes Hakiia must have brought him on the trip intending to kill him and eat him as part of the ritual. The brother he has just emotionally reclaimed becomes, at once, a deeper betrayal.

14. Link to the true villain
    The ritual materials connect Hakiia to the merchant prince behind the demon meat trade, revealing that Hakiia was meant to be part of a larger experiment in inherited power and that Hurkuzak is the unexpected successful result.

15. Capture, feast, and sequel hook
    Captain Vark drives Hurkuzak into the hands of the merchant prince, who prepares him as the centerpiece of a final harvest. Hurkuzak accepts Hakiia as part of himself without surrendering who he is, destroys the operation, learns the rite once preserved demon lineages and memory, and then the sequel hook lands: far away, someone who consumed a fragment of Hurkuzak hears two voices for the first time.
