## Character State

### Hurkuzak
- **Physical:** Dramatically improved from the consumption. Stronger, warmer, more alert than he should be. Enhanced senses (distance vision, hearing). Cold resistance. Grip strength increased. Body answering to different rules.
- **Injuries:** All previous injuries healed. No current wounds. Healing rate dramatically beyond normal.
- **Knowledge:** Complete picture. Knows: (1) the mechanism; (2) Hakiia's intent; (3) himself as "vessel"; (4) the merchant prince as patron/system-builder; (5) the patron's experimental history (prior sibling pairs, mostly failed); (6) WHY the patron's protocol fails (dominance-based, controlled, sterile — cannot produce love/grief/consent conditions); (7) WHY his own integration succeeded (mercy, love, willing death, prolonged intimacy); (8) Hakiia's reason for accepting (need to protect, not ambition). Now understands he is both proof and refutation of the patron's design. Does not know the patron's personal name or exact location. Traveling east with purpose — approaching, not fleeing. Has hatchet, knife, bow, provisions, plus all recovered materials/correspondence.
- **The Bleed stage:** LATE TRANSITION. Ch 30: completely silent. Ch 31: ambiguous pressure. Ch 32: changed tone, offers rather than corrects. Ch 33: CROSSING THRESHOLD — voice now contributes knowledge as certainties (patron's experimental history, Hakiia's motivations, design architecture). Not yet full sentences directed at Hurkuzak, but structural knowledge surfaces as fully formed certainties. Two presences increasingly aware of each other as separate entities sharing one body. First shared wanting (reckoning with the patron). Voice aligned with Hurkuzak's direction for the first time — alignment, not obedience or command. The dynamic is no longer master/subordinate or corrector/corrected. It is two selves orienting toward the same point.
- **Emotional state:** Holds three irreconcilable truths: (1) love was real from both directions; (2) Hakiia intended to kill him; (3) both brothers were materials in a system. None cancels the others. Direction has shifted from flight to approach — no longer prey but something the system didn't anticipate. Not revenge (too simple), not justice (too clean) — the need to confront the designer with the result. First time Hurkuzak has held an advantage in the story: the patron doesn't know what's coming.

### Hakiia
- **Status:** Dead. Killed by Hurkuzak in a mercy killing (Ch 9). Consent was explicit, lucid, and unquestionable.
- **Physical at death:** Both legs broken, right leg dying from infection/necrosis. Gash across forehead. Left shoulder injury. Right leg tissue dead from ankle upward. Body moved to cold alcove in the cabin (Ch 12). Preserved by cold. Furs over the face.
- **Final words/actions:** Said "please" — first time in his life. Acknowledged the cost to Hurkuzak ("I know that the weight of it does not end when my weight ends"). Said he trusted Hurkuzak to carry it. Gripped Hurkuzak's wrist at the moment of the knife — held on, then let go. Eyes stayed open after death; Hurkuzak closed them.
- **Voice presence:** N/A (alive)
- **Key memories surfaced:** N/A

### Captain Sella Vark
- **Physical:** Healthy. Stronger, faster, and more resilient than a normal human. Better night vision. Heals in days rather than weeks. Can work in extreme cold. All from years of consuming demon flesh in measured amounts.
- **Location:** Cassavon, a trade town. Rents a stone workspace behind a tanner's yard for processing.
- **Knowledge of Hurkuzak:** Has tracked him, investigated the bandit fight, shot him with a crossbow bolt, and observed his healing. Confirmed: capabilities exceed standard demon range. Healing factor is a multiple of the best she's seen. Blood scent is mixed (not standard demon, not human). Movement profile shows "installed" rather than inherent capabilities. Working hypothesis: sibling consumption. Does not know his name or specific history. Has a blood sample. Pursuing at professional distance.
- **Consumed properties:** Enhanced strength (folds heavy demon hide one-handed), speed, healing, cold resistance, night vision. Changes accumulated over years of professional consumption.
- **Professional position:** Respected hunter, best in her range. Works with factor Koss (southern trading house). Understands the system's hierarchy — she is the hands, the profit goes to those above her. Not bitter about it; exact.

### Orath Tessivane (the merchant prince)
- **Status:** Identified by title (Ch 32), not yet named or physically introduced.
- **Knowledge:** Known through correspondence as the patron who funded Hakiia's ritual attempt. Has conducted prior sibling-consumption experiments (inconsistent results). Operates above the flesh economy's visible hierarchy. System-builder who treats identity transfer as a technology to be refined.
- **Operations:** Recruits sibling pairs, supplies materials and protocol, assigns locations, evaluates agents. Prior experiments failed (personality erosion, composite instability, total failure). Hurkuzak is his accidental success — the protocol inverted.
- **Hurkuzak's knowledge of him:** Title only ("the merchant prince"). No name, no physical description. Supply chain runs east. The voice offers directional certainty.
