# Editorial Review: Chapter 37 — The Prince's Table

---

## Issue Log

### 1.
**Passage:** "The door opened and the man who entered was not what the architecture had prepared Hurkuzak to expect."
**Problem:** Solid opening. Clean, intriguing, inverts expectation immediately. The word "architecture" does double duty (literal building, figurative system). Strength noted.
**Priority:** N/A (strength)

### 2.
**Passage:** "The stronghold was a machine — brutal in purpose, refined in execution, built to process bodies with the impersonal efficiency of industry. The man who owned the machine should have matched it: hard, cold, the physical expression of a system that turned beings into product."
**Problem:** Explaining after showing. The first sentence establishes the stronghold. The second sentence tells the reader what Hurkuzak expected, which the opening line already conveyed ("not what the architecture had prepared Hurkuzak to expect"). We don't need "hard, cold, the physical expression of a system" spelled out — the reader is already imagining that expectation from sentence one.
**Why it weakens:** Two sentences of setup for the gap the reader already grasps. "Physical expression of a system that turned beings into product" is an essay clause stapled onto a character introduction.
**Revision approach:** Cut the second sentence entirely or compress to a fragment. The first sentence and the opening line together establish the expectation. Move immediately to the man who defies it.
**Priority:** High

### 3.
**Passage:** "Instead, the man who entered was gentle in his movements, unhurried, carrying himself with the particular ease of someone who has never needed to rush because the world has always arranged itself around his schedule."
**Problem:** "The particular ease of someone who has never needed to rush because the world has always arranged itself around his schedule" — the because-clause over-explains what "unhurried" and "ease" already convey. Also, "the man who entered" is the third occurrence of this phrase in three sentences.
**Why it weakens:** "Unhurried" is the observation. "Because the world has always arranged itself around his schedule" is the essay about the observation. Also, the repetition of "the man who entered" creates a stiff, formal rhythm.
**Revision approach:** Let "unhurried" do its work. Cut or radically compress the because-clause. Vary the sentence subject.
**Priority:** Medium

### 4.
**Passage:** "He was middle-aged. His hair was dark and well-kept. His face was lean and alert, the face of a man who paid attention to things because paying attention was a pleasure rather than a necessity."
**Problem:** The first two sentences are flat catalog — "He was X. His Y was Z." The third sentence recovers with a good character observation, but "the face of a man who paid attention to things because paying attention was a pleasure rather than a necessity" over-explains. "Lean and alert" already shows attentiveness; the because-clause is literary criticism of the adjective.
**Why it weakens:** "Middle-aged. Dark and well-kept." These are placeholder descriptions — they paint nothing specific. Then the third sentence explains what "alert" means.
**Revision approach:** Find one or two specific physical details that individuate Tessivane instead of generic catalog. Cut the because-clause on attention.
**Priority:** Medium

### 5.
**Passage:** "His clothing was excellent — tailored, clean, the kind of clothing that communicated wealth not through ostentation but through the quiet authority of fabric that fit because it had been made to fit."
**Problem:** Over-explaining the clothing. "Excellent — tailored, clean" is fine. But "the kind of clothing that communicated wealth not through ostentation but through the quiet authority of fabric that fit because it had been made to fit" is 27 words saying "his clothes fit well and looked expensive without being showy." The final clause — "fabric that fit because it had been made to fit" — is circular.
**Why it weakens:** The passage spends more words describing what the clothing communicates than what the clothing looks like. The reader gets a theory of wealth-signaling rather than a visual.
**Revision approach:** Cut everything after "tailored, clean." Or give one concrete visual detail — the color, the material, the cut — that implies the wealth without theorizing about it.
**Priority:** High

### 6.
**Passage:** "He was not tall. He was not imposing. He looked like what he was: a scholar with resources, a collector with taste, a man whose appetites had been educated into sophistication and whose sophistication had been funded into empire."
**Problem:** Mixed. "He was not tall. He was not imposing." — good, concise, subverts expectation. But the triple appositive ("a scholar with resources, a collector with taste, a man whose appetites...") is a stacked character summary that tells rather than shows. "Appetites had been educated into sophistication and whose sophistication had been funded into empire" is an epigram straining for effect — two whose-clauses chained with a chiasmus that draws attention to its own cleverness.
**Why it weakens:** The chiasmus is a display piece. It reads like a character essay, not close-third-person observation. Hurkuzak would not think in chiasmus.
**Revision approach:** Keep "He was not tall. He was not imposing." Cut or compress the triple appositive. If the sophistication-to-empire insight stays, deliver it once, plainly.
**Priority:** High

### 7.
**Passage:** "He carried two plates from the sideboard to the table with his own hands. He placed one at the head and one before Hurkuzak and the placing was courteous, domestic, the gesture of a host who takes pleasure in serving because the serving is a form of intimacy with the meal."
**Problem:** Explaining after showing. "He carried two plates... with his own hands" is the image — a powerful man serving personally. "The gesture of a host who takes pleasure in serving because the serving is a form of intimacy with the meal" is a 24-word gloss on a gesture that already spoke for itself.
**Why it weakens:** The image of the merchant prince carrying plates is striking precisely because it is unexpected. The explanation domesticates the surprise.
**Revision approach:** End at "courteous, domestic." Or cut even those adjectives — the action carries the meaning.
**Priority:** High

### 8.
**Passage:** "The plates held food that Hurkuzak's enhanced senses identified before his eyes could fully process the presentation: demon flesh, prepared with extraordinary care, rendered into a cuisine so refined that the brutality of the source material had been completely absorbed into the artistry of the preparation."
**Problem:** Overwriting. "Rendered into a cuisine so refined that the brutality of the source material had been completely absorbed into the artistry of the preparation" is 23 words of thematic thesis about the food. "Source material" and "artistry of the preparation" are clinical/aesthetic-theory language. Also, "completely absorbed" is an empty intensifier — "absorbed" is sufficient.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence tells the reader what the food means (brutality transformed into art) instead of showing what the food looks like, smells like, or is.
**Revision approach:** Show the dish. What does demon flesh look like when prepared by a master? The thematic point (brutality refined into culture) is the entire chapter's project — it doesn't need to be stated in the food description.
**Priority:** High

### 9.
**Passage:** "The smell was rich, complex, layered with spices and techniques that elevated the base material into something a person could consume not merely for strength but for pleasure."
**Problem:** Abstract sensory description. "Rich, complex, layered" are wine-review adjectives. "Techniques" is not something you smell. "Elevated the base material" repeats the previous sentence's thesis (brutality refined into art). "Not merely for strength but for pleasure" is the same thematic point again.
**Why it weakens:** Two consecutive sentences making the same argument (demon flesh elevated into cuisine) with abstract language instead of concrete sensory detail. The reader has no idea what this food actually smells like.
**Revision approach:** Pick specific spices, specific aromas. One concrete scent detail communicates more than "rich, complex, layered." Cut the thematic restatement.
**Priority:** High

### 10.
**Passage:** "The man sat at the head of the table. He looked at Hurkuzak with an expression that was not predatory but appreciative, the way a man who has spent years searching for a particular piece of art looks at the piece when he finally finds it — not with greed but with the satisfaction of confirmation."
**Problem:** The simile is apt (collector finding the piece) but then the sentence explains the simile: "not with greed but with the satisfaction of confirmation" re-glosses "not predatory but appreciative." The reader understood the comparison; the restatement is redundant.
**Why it weakens:** The art-collector simile does the work. The follow-up not-X-but-Y restates the same distinction in different words.
**Revision approach:** End at "when he finally finds it." The simile is clear.
**Priority:** Medium

### 11.
**Passage:** "The search had been real. The piece was real. The satisfaction was earned."
**Problem:** Three sentence fragments that explain the simile from the previous sentence. Each one annotates an element of the art-collector comparison. This is a third layer of explanation for a look Tessivane gave.
**Why it weakens:** "The satisfaction was earned" in particular is the author insisting on Tessivane's feelings rather than showing them through his behavior or words.
**Revision approach:** Cut all three sentences. The simile already conveyed this.
**Priority:** High

### 12.
**Passage:** "His voice was warm and educated, carrying the cadence of a man who had spoken to many people about many things and had found most of those conversations interesting."
**Problem:** "Carrying the cadence of a man who had spoken to many people about many things and had found most of those conversations interesting" — over-explains what "warm and educated" already established. The sentence builds a backstory for the voice rather than rendering the voice.
**Why it weakens:** The reader needs to hear Tessivane's voice through his dialogue. A 28-word biography of his conversational history is telling, not showing.
**Revision approach:** Cut the cadence clause. "His voice was warm and educated" is sufficient, and his dialogue will prove the rest.
**Priority:** Medium

### 13.
**Passage:** "He paused. The pause was not dramatic. It was the pause of a man who was organizing his thoughts because his thoughts deserved organization."
**Problem:** Over-characterizing a pause. Three sentences to explain a pause. "Because his thoughts deserved organization" is the author editorializing on Tessivane's self-regard through a beat of silence.
**Why it weakens:** The reader can interpret a pause. Three sentences of pause-analysis slow the scene to a crawl at the moment the character's name is about to land.
**Revision approach:** "He paused." Or cut entirely — the dialogue will convey pacing.
**Priority:** Medium

### 14.
**Passage:** "The name landed with the weight of accumulated knowledge — the fragments from the wreck, the protocols, the word vessel, the patron who funded experiments, the system-builder who sat above the flesh economy and directed its highest purposes."
**Problem:** Good. The catalog of prior references earns its length because each item is specific and previously established. The name-reveal is well-placed. Strength noted.
**Priority:** N/A (strength)

### 15.
**Passage:** "The name converted the architecture into a person and the person was sitting across the table from Hurkuzak and looking at him with genuine curiosity and the curiosity was more frightening than any threat because the curiosity was real and the realness meant that Tessivane did not see Hurkuzak as a prisoner to be broken but as a phenomenon to be understood and the understanding was what made him dangerous."
**Problem:** Run-on sentence / excessive and-chaining / explaining emotion. This is a single 80-word sentence with seven "and" connectors. It chains: architecture → person → sitting → looking → curiosity → frightening → real → phenomenon → understood → dangerous. Each clause explains the previous clause. "The curiosity was more frightening than any threat because the curiosity was real" — "curiosity" appears three times. "Real/realness" appears twice.
**Why it weakens:** The sentence's structure mirrors the breathless accumulation of realization, which has some rhetorical justification, but the repetition of "curiosity" and the daisy-chain of because-clauses reads as the author working through the logic on the page rather than having arrived at a conclusion before writing.
**Revision approach:** Break into 2-3 sentences. Cut repeated words. The key insight — that curiosity is more dangerous than hostility — deserves a clean, sharp delivery, not burial in an 80-word clause chain.
**Priority:** High

### 16.
**Passage:** "The eruption was physical: Hurkuzak's jaw clenched, his pulse climbed, his hands closed into fists inside the restraints with a force that tested the chemical suppression."
**Problem:** Good embodied writing. Physical specificity, escalating scale (jaw → pulse → hands → restraints). Strength noted.
**Priority:** N/A (strength)

### 17.
**Passage:** "The reaction was Hakiia's. The reaction was the rage and shame and recognition of a man confronting the person who had recruited him and used him and designed the experiment that had sent him into the mountains to kill his own brother."
**Problem:** "The reaction was Hakiia's" is strong. The second sentence over-explains it with a triple-and chain ("rage and shame and recognition") followed by another triple chain ("recruited him and used him and designed the experiment"). The entire second sentence is a biographical summary that explains the rage rather than letting the reader feel it through the physical reaction already described.
**Why it weakens:** The physical details (jaw clenched, pulse climbed, fists in restraints) already showed the reaction. The second sentence annotates those details with a character study.
**Revision approach:** Keep "The reaction was Hakiia's." Cut or radically compress the second sentence. The reader knows Hakiia's history with Tessivane — a brief flash is enough.
**Priority:** High

### 18.
**Passage:** "Hakiia had known this man. Had sat in a room with this man. Had listened to this man explain the protocol and the purpose and the expected outcome and had accepted the explanation and the purpose and the expectation because the acceptance had seemed like strength and the strength had seemed like survival and the survival had been the only thing Hakiia had ever understood as love."
**Problem:** Overwriting / daisy-chain logic / repetition. "The protocol and the purpose and the expected outcome and had accepted the explanation and the purpose and the expectation" — seven and-connectors in one sentence. "Purpose" appears twice. "Explanation/expectation/acceptance" chain creates an echo chamber. The final clause — "the acceptance had seemed like strength and the strength had seemed like survival and the survival had been the only thing Hakiia had ever understood as love" — is a three-link chain of seemed-like redirections.
**Why it weakens:** The rhetorical structure (A seemed like B, B seemed like C, C was D) is effective once. But it arrives after an already exhausting and-chain. The reader is processing too many linked abstractions. The insight about survival-as-love is powerful but buried.
**Revision approach:** Separate the physical memory ("Had sat in a room with this man") from the thematic insight (survival as love). The seemed-like chain is the strongest element — give it room by cutting the preceding clutter.
**Priority:** High

### 19.
**Passage:** "Tessivane noticed the reaction. He noticed it the way a scientist notices a result — with the careful attention of someone for whom observation is a professional skill."
**Problem:** "He noticed it the way a scientist notices a result" is a good simile. "With the careful attention of someone for whom observation is a professional skill" explains the simile. The reader understood "scientist notices a result" — they don't need the professional-observation gloss.
**Why it weakens:** Same pattern: simile followed by its own footnote.
**Revision approach:** End at "a result." Cut the rest.
**Priority:** Medium

### 20.
**Passage:** "'Ah,' he said. 'Both of you.'"
**Problem:** Excellent. Three words that confirm what the reader feared: Tessivane can read the integration. Chilling in its casualness. Strength noted.
**Priority:** N/A (strength)

### 21.
**Passage:** "'Your hands clenched, but the clenching was not yours — the pattern was different from your baseline physiological stress response. It was his. The integration is deep enough that his emotional reactions produce physical manifestation through your body without your conscious direction.'"
**Problem:** The dialogue is mostly in character for a researcher, but "baseline physiological stress response" is heavy clinical jargon even for a scholar in this world. "Produce physical manifestation through your body without your conscious direction" is a 10-word phrase for "move your body." Also, this dialogue is Tessivane explaining to Hurkuzak (and the reader) what the reader just saw happen — telling after showing.
**Why it weakens:** The reader watched Hurkuzak's jaw clench and fists ball. Tessivane explaining the mechanism is somewhat justified (it characterizes him as an analyst), but the length of the explanation dilutes the dramatic moment.
**Revision approach:** Trim. Tessivane's clinical observation can be shorter: "Your hands clenched, but the pattern was his, not yours. His reactions move through your body." The diagnosis character-beat is preserved without the lecture.
**Priority:** Medium

### 22.
**Passage:** "His voice was rough from disuse and sedation and the particular hoarseness of a man who has not spoken aloud in days because the person he has been speaking to lives inside his skull."
**Problem:** The first part ("rough from disuse and sedation") is clean. "The particular hoarseness of a man who has not spoken aloud in days because the person he has been speaking to lives inside his skull" is clever but self-conscious. The because-clause explains the joke, and "the particular hoarseness of a man who" is a construction the chapter has used repeatedly (see "the particular ease of someone who," "the pause of a man who," etc.).
**Why it weakens:** The construction "the [quality] of a man/person who [biographical explanation]" is this chapter's dominant sentence pattern. By this point it reads as a tic. Also, the wry observation about speaking to someone inside his skull is the kind of winking that the project instructions warn against.
**Revision approach:** "His voice was rough from disuse and sedation." The reader knows why he hasn't been talking.
**Priority:** Medium

### 23.
**Passage:** "'He volunteered to participate in a process that, had it succeeded as designed, would have made him something unprecedented. He understood the costs. He accepted them.'"
**Problem:** Strong dialogue. Tessivane's euphemistic precision ("process," "costs," "accepted") characterizes him efficiently. "He understood the costs. He accepted them." — clean, cold, devastating in context. Strength noted.
**Priority:** N/A (strength)

### 24.
**Passage:** "Tessivane's voice carried no apology and no cruelty. It carried the specific tone of a man who had thought carefully about the ethics of his work and had arrived at conclusions he was willing to defend."
**Problem:** First sentence is good — "no apology and no cruelty" is a precise negative description. Second sentence explains the first. "The specific tone of a man who had thought carefully about the ethics of his work and had arrived at conclusions he was willing to defend" — another instance of the "the [quality] of a man who" construction. This time it is telling the reader about Tessivane's ethical self-regard rather than letting his words demonstrate it.
**Why it weakens:** His words already demonstrate it. "He understood the costs. He accepted them." — that already showed a man who has arrived at defended conclusions. The narration doesn't need to label the tone.
**Revision approach:** Keep "His voice carried no apology and no cruelty." Cut the second sentence.
**Priority:** Medium

### 30.
**Passage:** "Hurkuzak did not touch the plate. The refusal was not principled. It was physical — the idea of eating demon flesh prepared as cuisine in a room built for the celebration of consumption while restrained in a chair by the man who had designed his brother's death produced a revulsion that went beyond the moral and into the cellular."
**Problem:** Explaining emotion. "The refusal was not principled. It was physical" is clean. But then the sentence explaining why it's physical runs 46 words and ends with "went beyond the moral and into the cellular," which is an abstract explanatory phrase. The reader doesn't need "went beyond the moral and into the cellular" — the 40-word situational description already established why the refusal was physical.
**Why it weakens:** The long subordinate clause (eating... in a room... while restrained... by the man who...) does good cumulative work, but "went beyond the moral and into the cellular" is an editorial label tacked onto the end.
**Revision approach:** End the sentence before "produced a revulsion." The stacking of circumstances is the revulsion. Or end simply: "the idea produced a revulsion he could not have overcome if he'd wanted to."
**Priority:** Medium

### 31.
**Passage:** "Tessivane accepted the refusal without comment, the way a courteous host accepts any guest's preference."
**Problem:** "The way a courteous host accepts any guest's preference" — another "the way a [type of person]" simile. This one works because the irony is strong (a courteous host who eats people), but the construction is overused in this chapter.
**Why it weakens:** Minor. The irony carries it. But the pattern saturation means the reader may not register the irony because the sentence shape is familiar.
**Revision approach:** Keep, but be aware of pattern fatigue. If other instances of this construction are cut, this one gets to breathe.
**Priority:** Low

### 32.

Tessivane eating demon flesh casually is already showing the normalization; calling it "communion with his own philosophy" is the author writing a thesis about Tessivane's character.
**Why it weakens:** The casual eating is powerful. The communion-with-philosophy gloss converts the image into an essay.
**Revision approach:** End at "casual, practiced." Maybe one concrete detail about how he eats — the way he handles the utensil, the unhurried pace. Cut the philosophy line.
**Priority:** High

### 40.

Tessivane is thorough, which is in character, but three restatements in one speech is the author's over-explanation habit wearing Tessivane's mask.

### 41.

### 42.
**Passage:** "'I intend to understand it. I intend to map the architecture of your integration — how the two of you coexist, how memory surfaces, how instinct operates across the boundary, how will and identity interact. I intend to produce a model. And from the model, I intend to develop a method that replicates the result.'"

The repetition is somewhat justified as characterization (Tessivane's methodical certainty), but the four-how list ("how the two of you coexist, how memory surfaces, how instinct operates across the boundary, how will and identity interact") is a research proposal rather than dialogue. **Revision approach:** Keep two "I intend" statements. Cut or compress the four-how list — "I intend to map the architecture of your integration" already implies the specifics.
**Priority:** Medium

### 43.
**Passage:** "The voice pushed against the inside of Hurkuzak's skull with a fury that was entirely Hakiia's — the fury of hearing his own worst act described as a data point, his own monstrous choice analyzed as a variable, the love he had twisted into violence reduced to a parameter in a model that a man at a table planned to optimize while eating the flesh of beings like both of them."

The escalation — data point → variable → parameter → optimize — mirrors the reductive dehumanization of Tessivane's language turned back on him. The final image ("while eating the flesh of beings like both of them") lands because it grounds the abstraction in physical horror. However, "data point... variable... parameter... model... optimize" is five research-jargon terms in one sentence.
**Why it weakens:** Minor. The jargon-escalation is intentional and mostly effective. The sentence could be tightened but works.
**Revision approach:** Consider cutting one link in the chain — four instead of five. Otherwise, leave it.
**Priority:** Low

### 44.

Then "the particular weight of a statement made by two people speaking through one mouth" explains the explanation. The reader understands duality from 36 chapters of Bleed development.
**Why it weakens:** "You want to industrialize it" is a perfect line. The three-sentence annotation dilutes it.

## Overall Diagnosis

Chapter 37 introduces Orath Tessivane, the novel's system-builder antagonist, and the chapter's central dramatic engine — a dialogue between predator-as-scholar and prey-as-phenomenon — is strong. The scene's architecture works: Tessivane serves food, names himself, explains his project, reveals the scope of his experiments, and inadvertently gives Hurkuzak and Hakiia the information they need to begin planning resistance. The Bleed moment ("I sat where you are sitting. He said the same things. I believed him.") is the chapter's emotional peak and among the novel's best lines. Tessivane's dialogue is largely well-crafted — his clinical euphemisms ("contribute," "protocol," "failure modes," "the nature of research") are chilling and in character.

The chapter's central weakness is **compulsive annotation**. Nearly every action, look, gesture, tone, and pause is followed by a subordinate clause or appositional phrase that explains what it reveals about the character performing it. The dominant construction — "the [quality] of a man/person who [biographical explanation]" — appears at least twelve times. This construction converts showing into telling: instead of rendering Tessivane's behavior and trusting the reader to draw conclusions, the narration draws the conclusions for them, repeatedly, in the same syntactic shape.

Secondary weaknesses include: stacked explanations of the food that make thematic arguments instead of providing sensory detail; the collector simile used twice with diminishing returns; three separate narrator annotations explaining the Bleed's dual-voice quality; and a 70-word sentence near the end that recapitulates the chapter's theme as a taboo-to-institution-to-philosophy chain.

The chapter's best writing is its dialogue and its most compressed moments — the four-beat "Contribute. Die. Be eaten. Become material." sequence, the three-sentence Hakiia confession, and the closing paragraph's "architecture of appetite." A revision that strips the annotation apparatus and trusts these moments would produce a significantly tighter, more powerful chapter.

## Pattern Summary

The chapter's dominant weakness is **characterization through annotation rather than observation**. The narrator does not describe what people do and let the reader interpret; the narrator describes what people do and then explains what the doing reveals about who they are. The syntactic vehicle for this is the "the [quality] of a man/person who [explanation]" construction, which appears 12+ times and is the chapter's signature tic.

Additional patterns:

- **Double explanation (narrator + dialogue):** The narrator explains a moment, then a character explains the same moment in dialogue, or vice versa. The Bleed duality is explained by the narrator and then by Tessivane in nearly identical words.
- **Thematic thesis statements in narration:** The narrator periodically writes essay sentences about what the scene means ("the place where the private taboo had been turned into a public institution and the institution had been turned into a philosophy").
- **Abstract sensory description:** The food is described in terms of what it represents rather than what it looks, smells, or tastes like. "Rich, complex, layered" are evaluations, not perceptions.
- **Simile recycling:** The collector simile, the appreciative look, the scientific observer — each comparison is used 2-3 times with slightly different wording.
- **And-chains as connective tissue:** Sentences regularly chain 4-7 clauses with "and," creating breathless run-ons that bury their strongest elements.

## Revision Plan (Priority Order)

1. **Eliminate 9-10 of the 12 "the [quality] of a man who" constructions.** Keep 2-3 where the construction earns its weight. Replace the rest with physical detail, direct statement, or silence. This single pass will transform the chapter's rhythm.

2. **Cut every explaining-after-showing tail.** Identify every sentence that follows a strong image or moment and restates its meaning. Cut it. This applies to: the plate-serving, the collector look, the appreciative attention, the Hakiia reaction, the Bleed confession.

3. **Rewrite the food description with concrete sensory detail.** Cut the thematic arguments about brutality-into-art. Show the reader the dish — color, texture, arrangement, specific aromas. Let the horror of refined demon cuisine emerge from specificity, not thesis.

4. **Consolidate Bleed duality explanation.** The reader needs the observation/contempt duality explained zero times by the narrator. Let Tessivane's dialogue carry it — his clinical analysis of the dual voice is in character and advances the scene. Cut the narrator's version.

5. **Let Hakiia's confession stand alone.** Cut everything between "I believed him." and the closing paragraphs' shift to resistance-planning. The three sentences are the chapter's emotional peak and require silence, not annotation.

6. **Break the post-name-reveal run-on.** Split the 80-word sentence into 2-3 sentences. The key insight (curiosity is more frightening than threat) deserves clean, sharp delivery.

7. **Cut the second collector simile** (paragraph before Tessivane leaves). Keep the first instance. If "the singular became the systematic" must survive, deliver it independently.

8. **Compress the closing thematic summary.** Cut the taboo → institution → philosophy chain. "The food on Hurkuzak's plate cooled, untouched" transitions directly to the Bleed.

9. **Tighten Tessivane's introduction.** Replace generic catalog descriptions with 1-2 specific physical details. Cut the chiastic epigram. Cut or compress the clothing paragraph.

10. **Break and-chains throughout.** Reduce the 7-and Hakiia memory sentence, the 5-and name-reaction sentence, and similar run-ons. Keep 1-2 where the breathless rhythm is justified; break the rest into shorter, sharper units.
